Poisonous Concoction of Bouncing Pathfinder League Starter for Path of Exile Mirage 3.28

by havoc616
Mirage 3.28Last Updated: March 31, 2026

This Poisonous Concoction of Bouncing Pathfinder League Starter Build Guide teaches you the most efficient way to level your character, acquire your first 4 Voidstones, and scale into Endgame.

Poisonous Concoction of Bouncing is an unarmed attack skill that throws a single bottle which explodes in an area on impact. Afterwards, the skill bounces in random directions, based on the number of chains, which can be further increased by additional projectiles. The result is a single bottle will hit and poison the same enemy 5 to 10 times or more!

During the campaign, you level with Poisonous Concoction, and swap to Poisonous Concoction of Bouncing as soon as possible, most often after acquiring the gem from Merciless Lab.

Early on, you survive with Life, Evasion and strong positioning. During Atlas Progression, you upgrade your survivability with even more Evasion, Energy Shield via Hybrid Gear, Ghost Dance for burst damage protection, Spell Suppression, and Cast when Damage Taken + Immortal Call.

This build doesn't require any Unique items to function, but does benefit from Uniques such as The Embalmer, Snakebite, Dendrobate, Fury Valve or Betrayal's Sting.

Resistances

You do not need specific resistances on any item, just get the required total resistances amongst all gear combined. You need to cap your Resistances at 75% unless otherwise specified in the guide!

Main Damage
Auras
Mobility
  • Poisonous Concoction of Bouncing & Plague Bearer are both your primary clear and single target combo:
    • For clear: attack into each pack and run through them with Plague Bearer and Withering Step active.
      • Note: You need a Quartz Flask or any other source of Phasing when Withering Step is not active.
    • For single target: continuously attack the target with Poisonous Concoction of Bouncing and activate Plague Bearer when it reaches its Maximum Plague Value.
    • Debuff the target with Despair.
  • Withering Step instantly applies Wither Stacks, increasing the damage enemies take, but is primarily comboed with Plague Bearer for clear.
  • Sniper's Mark + Mark On Hit Support significantly improves your single target damage by splitting your Poisonous Concoction projectiles while generating Frenzy Charges with the appropriate Mark Mastery.
  • Malevolence is used to increase your damage by providing % more Damage over Time & % increased Skill Effect Duration.
  • Grace improves your survivability by significantly improving your Evasion rating.
  • Flesh and Stone reduces damage taken by Enemies within its radius when in Sand Stance.
  • A low level, Precision is used to cap your hit chance. It also assists with activating Precise Technique while leveling.
  • While not explicitly an aura, Cast when Damage Taken + Immortal Call is used to increase your survivability against burst damage.
  • In the Endgame, Petrified Blood is used to significantly increase both your Max Hit & Effective Hit Pool.
  • Flame Dash and Shield Charge are your primary mobility methods.
  • Optionally: Frostblink can be used instead of Flame Dash, which can be comboed with Shield Charge by using it at the end of the Shield Charge movement.

How to Ascend

Learn how to find the Trials of Ascendancy and complete The Lord's Labyrinth to earn your Ascensions with our Labyrinth Guide!

  • Master Toxicist is your first ascendancy node, increasing both your clear and single target damage after Normal Lab.
    • Note: You can ascend as a Deadeye and allocate Gathering Winds from Normal Lab to progress through the campaign faster. However, this option is only recommended for experienced players.
  • Nature's Reprisal is allocated in Cruel Lab to significantly increase your single target damage by inflicting Wither on Hit and increasing the effect of all Wither Debuffs.
  • Nature's Adrenaline is allocated in Merciless Lab, significantly improving your Flask Uptime, thus improving your mobility, clear speed and survivability.
  • Nature's Boon is taken in Uber Lab to increase your Flask Effectiveness.

How to Progress

After finishing the campaign, you begin interacting with the Atlas of Worlds. Learn more about how to progress your Atlas check out our Atlas progression guide! Or head over to our Boss section to learn how to defeat the Eldritch Horrors you encounter while exploring the Atlas.

  • Look to solve Intelligence, Dexterity and Strength requirements on gear. If you find yourself short you can spec into Attribute Notables as temporary solutions.
    • You can use your Faustus to respec if a new skill or gear upgrade leaves you unable to use your skills/items.
  • Campaign: Click through the campaign embed to follow the recommended passive tree progression. During the early Acts, your Poisonous Concoction damage is mostly Hit based. As you progress, your damage eventually becomes primarily Poison based.
    • It's strongly recommended to refarm zones when leveling with Poisonous Concoction since you are highly mobile but low in damage early on:
      • Act 2: Northern Forest
      • Act 3: Docks
      • Act 4: Dried Lake
      • Act 5: Chamber of Innocence
    • Consider allocating Field Medicine during Act 2 & 3 Progression to improve your Flask Sustain.
    • Consider allocating Weathered Hunter earlier if required to meet the accuracy requirements for Precise Technique.
    • Note: If you are an experienced player, and wish to complete the campaign approximately 30 minutes faster, then refer to this Explosive Concoction Deadeye Leveling Build Guide.
  • 2 Stone: After acquiring Poisonous Concoction of Bouncing from Merciless Lab, ensure you've crafted a Divine Life Flask or two with % Increased Amount Recovered then begin your Atlas Progression. As you approach the Pinnacle Bosses, ensure you've allocated Inveterate and the Chance to Suppress Spell Damage is Lucky mastery for additional survivability. Aim for a combined total of 3K+ Life & Energy Shield and 1Million+ DPS for a smooth experience.
  • 4 Stone: Your primary passive tree goal during 4 Stone progression is to cap your Spell Suppression. Capped Spell Suppression significantly improves your survivability versus the final 2 Pinnacle Bosses: Maven & Uber Elder. Additionally, aim for a combined total of 3.6K to 4K+ Life & Energy Shield and 2Million to 3Million+ DPS to easily defeat the final bosses.
  • Endgame: Your endgame passive tree is dependent on your gear, thus your endgame tree may differ from the example provided. Typically this means acquiring more stats on gear, allowing you to drop passive tree nodes or masteries, such as Spell Suppression or Accuracy.
    • Note: Its recommended to switch to a Viper Strike of Mamba Pathfinder after completing the Endgame Gear Set and farming enough currency as necessary. That said, additional aspirational endgame scaling options are provided as a courtesy for those that want to scale the build further.
  • Aspirational: There are many viable high investment build options for an endgame Pathfinder. The example provided is a DoT Capped, Lucky Max Block Mapper and Bosser that uses a Progenesis with a large amount of % increased Flask Effect for a massive amount of survivability. Additionally, the build uses:
    • Fury Valve
    • Snakebite
    • Olesya's Delight
    • Ralakesh's Impatience
    • Dying Sun

Offensive Scaling

  • Your Life Flask is your most important item for scaling your damage. The #1 Priority is a Divine Life Flask with as much Life Recovery as possible.
  • % Increased Life Recovery from Flasks: Field Medicine, Herbalism
  • % Increased Flask Effect
  • % Increased Damage: % Increased Chaos Damage, % Increased Damage over Time
  • % Increased DoT Multi: % Increased Damage over Time Multiplier, % Increased Chaos Damage over Time Multiplier, % Increased Damage over Time Multiplier for Poison
  • % Faster DoT: Poisons you inflict deal Damage % Faster, Damaging Ailments deal damage % Faster
  • % Increased Attack Speed
  • Additional Projectiles: Multishot, Fury Valve, Dying Sun

Defensive Scaling

  • Sufficient +#% to Fire, Cold and Lightning Resistance to cap your Resistances
  • Evasion Rating
  • Maximum Energy Shield on Hybrid Evasion/Energy Shield Items to use Ghost Dance for burst damage protection
  • Cast when Damage Taken Support + Immortal Call
  • A strong Divine Life Flask with Corrupting Blood Immunity.
  • Any flask with Freeze Immunity

Resistances

You can get Resistances wherever you want and they do not have to be on specific items. Resistances are incredibly important though. DO NOT forget about them when choosing which items to craft or buy! Make sure to check your Resistances in your Character Sheet (C, Defense Tab) whenever you switch your gear around. To learn more about defences in Path of Exile, check out our guide here!

Gear in this section is easier to craft if you obtain a Fractured base. This base 'locks' a modifier, so you can use  Essences  and other crafting methods to obtain a good item. You can search for fractured modifiers on trade by inputting "~fractured (name of modifier)" in the modifier search box. If you want to try out how to craft your final items, the Craft of Exile simulator is an amazing tool to plan them without wasting in-game currency.

  • During the campaign, your primary goals are to equip:
    • The Highest Level Life Flask you can equip, ideally with % Increased Amount Recovered
      • Level 12 Greater Life Flask
      • Level 18 Grand Life Flask
      • Level 24 Giant Life Flask
      • Level 30 Colossal Life Flask
      • Level 36 Sacred Life Flask
      • Level 42 Hallowed Life Flask
      • Level 50 Sanctified Life Flask
      • Level 60 Divine Life Flask (Best in Slot)
    • Any Shield that improves your DPS
    • 25% to 30% Movement Speed Boots
    • Life & Resistance Gear
    • Flat Damage Rings, Amulet or Gloves (Low Levels Only)
    • Accuracy Rings, Amulet, Shield, Gloves or Helmet
  • Acquisition of these items comes from either monster drops, using currency on normal bases, or gambling from Faustus.
  • Farm Poisonous Concoction of Bouncing from Merciless Lab or buy one from Trade before progressing to Maps.
    • Note: Consider running a few extra Merc Labs to both Level and Quality your Plague Bearer. Doing so significantly increases your Atlas progression speed and single target damage.
  • Upon defeating Act 10 Kitava, use the Gambling Vendor to upgrade any weaker item drops from earlier acts, primarily focusing on basic resistance capping. The life resistance gear you equip here is viable for defeating Eater and Exarch.
  • As you progress through the Atlas, continue to loot gear with Accuracy to cap your hit chance, and optionally with Suppression for more survivability. Ideally these upgrades are finalised before reaching Red Maps.
  • Around T11 Maps, gamble the gold you've acquired on Hybrid Evasion/Energy Shield body armours if you haven't acquired one already.
    • Note: if you find a 5L Evasion body armour during progression you can equip that as well. However, ensure you have 100 to 300+ total energy shield to effectively use Ghost Dance.
  • If you're playing Trade League, consider the following early Uniques:
    • The Embalmer for a Pseudo 5L
    • Dendrobate

  • Moving from 2 Stones to 4 Stones, your goals are the following:
    • Divine Life Flasks with 50%+ Increased Amount Recovered
    • 100% Spell Suppression
    • Reaching Level 90+
    • Generally upgraded stats on the rest of your items.
  • Any Chaos Large Cluster Jewel
    • Harvest Reforge Chaos for any combo of 2 Notables
  • Any Projectile Medium Cluster Jewels
    • Roll for Follow-Through with Alterations
  • Helmet
    • Acquire a Hybrid EV/ES Base
    • Use Shrieking Essence of Loathing until you hit an item with Life, Accuracy and optionally: Suppression
      • Note: You can use a helmet with No Life rolls if it also has a ton of Energy Shield
  • Gloves
    • Use Screaming Essence of Zeal until you hit an item with Suppression
    • Craft Life or Resist if necessary; otherwise, craft % increased Damage during any Flask Effect
  • Gamble for a 5L Hybrid Evasion/Energy Shield body armour if you haven't acquired one already.
    • Note: if you find a 5L Evasion body armour during progression you can equip that as well. However, ensure you have 100 to 300+ total energy shield to effectively use Ghost Dance.
    • When crafting Hybrid Evasion/Energy Shield gear, such as Sadist Garb, consider using a Woe Essence to guarantee a flat Energy Shield modifier.
  • Rings & Amulets
    • Craft Non-Channelling Skills have -# to Total Mana Cost to reduce your Mana Costs
    • Optionally, craft +1 to Minimum Endurance Charge on a Ring or Amulet for Endurance Charge Generation while mapping
    • Anoint your Amulet with Toxic Strikes to save passive tree skill points
  • Use the trade site or run Abyss in search of a Stygian Vise and a Searching Eye Jewel.
    • Use a Rage Essence to roll the belt.
    • Use Alterations, Augments, and Regals to roll the jewel. You can also use Harvest reforge.
    • If you don't need to craft a Life or a Resistance Suffix, craft the veiled modifier Regenerate 150 Energy shield per second while a Rare or Unique Enemy is Nearby to improve your ES Recovery.
  • Consider adding Eater or Exarch implicits to your gear with Lesser Eldritch Ember or Lesser Eldritch Ichor as depicted in the embed. This is the last step once you've equipped strong enough items for this stage.

Shock Nova of Procession Archmage Hierophant League Starter for Path of Exile Mirage 3.28

by Goratha
Mirage 3.28Last Updated: March 29, 2026

Advertisement

This website is supported by ads.

The Shock Nova of Procession Archmage Hierophant is one of the fastest spell casters in the game, covering the screen with Novas and obliterating enemies. This ability has massive potential with overlapping Shock Novas and has a satifsying playstyle that rewards good positioning. With the Archmage Support, mana gives both offense and defense making this character scale very well into the endgame where you have absurd cast speed.

Early on progress with Firestorm and Armageddon Brand to breeze through the campaign. Stack mana to gain a big pool through Mind Over Matter and damage with Archmage Support and Arcane Cloak. Once you reach the endgame you switch over to Shock Nova and start blasting.

Dual Wielding wands adds a lot of damage early at the cost of some defense. Later on you transition into more a block-based setup with Pledge of Hands. Pledge of Hands has Greater Spell Echo Support gem built-in, which synergizes very well with Shock Nova, giving it a massive damage boost, this combined with block makes the character very well rounded.

Resistances

You do not need specific resistances on any item, just get the required total resistances amongst all gear combined. You need to cap your Resistances at 75% unless otherwise specified in the guide!

Skills

Rolling Magma
Firestorm
Shock Nova of Procession
  • From level 1 to level 28 you use Rolling Magma as your clear skill.
  • Rolling Magma works well with Elemental Proliferation Support and Combustion Support supports, this gives it good clear.
  • Add in Flame Wall along with Holy Flame Totem to give the build some nice single target and a meat shield for bosses to target.
  • Shield Charge linked with Momentum Support and Faster Attacks Support should be used for early mobility along with either Flame Dash or Frostblink, whichever you prefer to use.
  • Once you hit level 16, add in Herald of Ice and Herald of Thunder, this gives you a little bit more damage early on.
  • When you reach level 28 you can swap Rolling Magma for Armageddon Brand, this uses the exact same gem links, and is used for clearing.
  • Make sure to keep an eye out for any flat damage to spells on weapons and gear, this makes the leveling process much smoother.
  • Firestorm becomes your single target, it scales very well with Archmage Support and Unleash Support.
  • Once you start using the Archmage Support support gem, drop both Herald of Ice and Herald of Thunder.
  • Combine Arcane Cloak with Automation at this point, it gives a big damage buffer and a ton of flat lightning damage based on your maximum mana.
  • Use Sigil of Power, for tanky Rares and bosses. Then stand within it and cast to build stages. These stages give you extra lightning damage and reduces damage taken from enemies.
  • At level 38 you get Eternal Blessing Support, this allows you to use one aura without costing reservation, with the downside being you can only use one aura.
  • Link Purity of Elements with Eternal Blessing Support, this gives you immunity to ailments and a decent amount of all resistances, making gearing much easier early on.
  • Once you finish campaign you should drop both Firestorm and Armageddon Brand, and equip the builds main skill, Shock Nova.
  • This skill works best with Frostblink and Dual-Wielding early, so at this point you should look to drop Shield Charge.
  • Spell Echo Support is a must have support gem, it benefits this skill much more than anything other, and eventually Greater Spell Echo Support is used through Pledge of Hands. Keep in mind Spell Echo Support and Greater Spell Echo Support do NOT stack and Pledge of Hands has Greater Spell Echo Support built in to the item.
  • The playstyle for Shock Nova is simple. Aim your cursor in the direction you want to cast and hold down right click. This ability has absurd cast speed and an interesting overlapping mechanic.
  • Shock Nova works best against large bosses, but if you can position properly, it overlaps even on small targets. Generally this means you need to stand a small distance away from the monster, this is something that changes based on your AoE. It feels very rewarding once you master it.
  • Use Sigil of Power and Frost Shield anytime you need to stand still for single target, this makes you far more tanky and allows for face tanking abilities you would otherwise die to.
  • Much later on you can swap Purity of Elements for Wrath, once you solve resistances on gear.

Ascendancy

Mirage 3.28
Shock Nova of Procession Archmage Hierophant League Starter
  • Take Conviction of Power from Normal Lab. This gives you 4 minimum Endurance and Power charges. Endurance charges grant a decent amount of mitigation and Power charges give critical strike chance, and while this isn't a critical strike build it still benefits from Elemental Overload, which is a big multiplier on the tree that triggers on critical.
  • From Cruel Labyrinth take Divine Guidance, at this point you should have a decent amount of mana nodes. This scales damage from those, as well as a bit more of damage taken from mana before life, which stacks with Mind Over Matter.
  • Merciless Labyrinth you take Sanctuary of Thought. This gives a massive amount of AoE and mana gained as energy shield. AoE scales very well with Shock Nova, it makes it go further and also increases the circle radius. This does change the overlapping distance, so make sure to pay attention to that.
  • Last but not least, take Arcane Blessing from Uber Labyrinth. Arcane Blessing scales from Arcane Surge effect on the tree, making it a very insane damage buff.

Passives

Mirage 3.28
Shock Nova of Procession Archmage Hierophant League Starter
  • In the early campaign, grab Precision as soon as possible, this grants Attack Speed and Cast Speed, which is very important for both Shield Charge and Rolling Magma. Next, grab Explosive Impact along with the Fire Mastery 100% increased damage with hits against ignited enemies. Make sure you have Elemental Proliferation Support on Rolling Magma as you need chance to ignite for this to work. Next work towards Elemental Overload, a keystone that grants a big damage buff. After that start working on Mana nodes as soon as you start using Arcane Cloak and Archmage Support. Ash, Frost and Storm along with it's mastery 15% chance to invert damage is another massive boost to damage.
  • Near the end of the campaign look to grab Light Eater along with it's mastery for 5% instant leech. Leech is one of the builds main defensive mechanics.
  • As you start entering maps, look to grab Magebane, Reflexes, and Instinct along with chance to supress is lucky. Evasion and Suppression is the main defensive mechanic for duel-wielding until you get Pledge of Hands.
  • Once you have Pledge of Hands respec out of Suppresion and Evasion nodes and start putting points into Staff Block nodes such as Enigmatic Defence, Safeguard, and Steelwood Stance.
  • The jewel nodes in the middle near Scion start are very important for endgame scaling, these work particularly well with the unique jewel Unnatural Instinct, and The Light of Meaning. Militant Faith is also quite good here.

You can get Resistances wherever you want and they do not have to be on specific items. Resistances are incredibly important though.   • During the campaign, try to get life and Resistances. Keep an eye out for Berek's Grip or Valako's Sign for Lightning Leech.
  • Make sure to craft flat damage to spells early on from Solaris level 2 recipe if you do not have a unique leveling weapon! Ashcaller is a good early game leveling weapon if you can get one.
  • Honourhome can also be a decent item to keep an eye out for early on as it helps with mana costs.
  • In the campaign, you should be focused on Evasion/ES Hybrid gear, keep an eye out for +dexterity rolls as you need some to wear better bases.
  • Cast Speed rings, amulet, and weapons go a long way in making your early progression smoother.
  • As you enter the endgame and begin mapping, your first goal is to cap your resistances and meet Attribute requirements.
  • Mindspiral is a very solid helmet option that grants a lot of flat mana and the very strong mana gained as energy shield modifier.
  • Annoint Mind Drinker on an amulet for easy mana sustain while mapping.
  • Keep an eye out for gear that has %suppression rolls and as much flat mana as possible. Mana scales both your offense and defense.
  • Gear needs a dexterity requirement to roll for suppression and an intelligence requirement to roll for mana.
  • Make sure you keep upgrading your chest piece as this is the majority of your early mapping defensive stats. Mana is not as important on this slot, focus on getting as much Evasion/ES as possible and try not to get any life modifiers, so you can make use of the 15% maximum life mastery on the tree.
  • Look to get Profane Wands with as much flat mana, spell damage, and cast speed as possible.
  • One of the best ways to scale the character into the endgame is the unique item Pledge of Hands. This item is amazing for Shock Nova of Procession as it has built in Greater Spell Echo Support, an amazing damage link. Once you equip this item make sure to replace the normal Spell Echo Support gem as it no longer functions.
  • Once you swap over to Pledge of Hands, you focus on Armour/ES gear instead of Evasion/ES, and you drop Suppression nodes on the tree in favor of Block nodes. This makes you feel a lot tankier while mapping in general.
  • Foulborn Berek's Respite is a good mapping option that helps clear a lot by exploding shocked enemies. Link Elemental Proliferation Support to Frostblink to spread shocks and enable massive screen wide clearing.
  • Foulborn Kikazaru is another good unique to look out for as it grants far more flat mana than normally possible on a ring, scaling based on your level.
  • Foulborn Kalisa's Grace makes mana sustain FAR easier, and allows you to drop Inspiration Support for another good damage support.
  • There is two main ways to scale damage even further. Invert the Rules Support with the Invert Elemental Mastery or Anathema.
  • Invert has a chance to "flip" enemy resistance making it a huge damage increase. To make use of this is simple, just don't reduce enemy monster resistance by using curses or exposure as it becomes a downside when it flips. Lightning Penetration Support is still good with this, as it doesn't apply to the enemy itself.
  • This is the recommended way to play with Pledge of Hands version of the build.
  • Anathema is the other option, this is a unique ring that grants you curses equal to your power charges, in this case you get 4. This is the highest boss damage variant but it requires you to sacrifice a ring slot and at a minimum 4 gem slots.
  • There is 4 ways to apply these curses. The easiest by far is to use the unique item Kitava's Thirst, this simply casts the curses when you spend mana, however this item has very bad defensive stats compared to Mindspiral, and even reduces your cast speed.
  • The next option is to use either Bane or Arcanist Brand to trigger the curses. Bane applies all curses linked to it in one cast, at the downside of reducing the curses effect, it's a nice middle-ground.
  • Arcanist Brand is a decent option for bossing, it rotates all linked spells, but it has delay so it's bad for mapping.
  • In this build guide the recommended way of doing it is by using the Focus helmet modifier, which triggers all spells when you use Focus. Focus is a skill granted whenever you have any item with the keyword "Focus" on it, in this case triggering socketed skills.
  • Anathema is the recommended way to play with the Dual-Wielding min/max set-up, but it requires much more set-up.

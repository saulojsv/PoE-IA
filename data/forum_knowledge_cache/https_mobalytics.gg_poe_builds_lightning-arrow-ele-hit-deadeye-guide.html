How it Works

Commonly asked Question/Build explanation.

Tattos can be movement speed, projectile speed, spell suppression or accuracy if needed. (remember proj speed is damage because of your bow mastery)

---Elemental hit vs lightning arrow vs Tornado shot
Ele hit is by far the best option at low budget because it's the only bow skill that provides a massive amount of flat damage, which is a stat you lack on low budget/league start.

Lightning arrow ends up feeling a lot better to play once you actually have a really good bow/hyrris to makeup for the low flat, it's also better for phys convert when you swap to a Mirror bow.

Tornado shot is generally the worse clear out of all 3 nowadays, but provides the highest single target option out of all 3. Do note, this skill feels really bad on low budget.

If you do want to play tornado shot, I generally advice to wait until you acquire a +2 bow, or the clear might feel quite bad. It's still a fine option to swap earlier if you're boss rushing and don't need clear.

---Pantheon:
soul of the brine king until you're ailment immune(100% chance to avoid elemental ailments or purity of elements), after you can swap to solaris or lunaris.

Minor pantheon is either Ralakesh or Shakari, depending on if you're weaker to bleed or poison.

---Bandit
Alira if you need res, otherwise kill all for +1 passive point.

---Deadeye or Warden?
You only play warden when you need very high single target damage, usually this scenario is doing t17/nightmare maps, as the bosses can be extremely tanky.

For atlas prog, 4 stone and t16/t16.5 mapping, deadeye is just the superior choice. +2 arrows is extremely valuable when starting out because you don't have any additional +arrows on bow/quiver yet. Tailwind/chain is also extremely strong.

And eventually When you can get a ff/ff, than warden loses a lot of value, since you can get oath of spring or avatar of the wilds and have massive single target damage on deadeye.

So keep in mind here, the Warden setup I have is pure single target (taking point blank, no fledging). Of course you could drop point blank if you already have enough damage.


---Trinity Support
A common misconception with trinity is needing to deal equal dmg with 3 elements. You only need 2 elements to get max effect ouf of trinity.

Ele hit is very easy to get trinity working. If you're using anger and have a very high fire dmg bow with no other element, than you might run into issues, but it's unlikely to happen. If that happens, just replace anger with another 50% auras (grace or haste).

You'll know if you have too much of 1 element, because that element bar wont be filling up within the trinity UI.


---Inspired learning, do I need it/what's the purpose?
This unique jewel just serves as a mini-hh and it's only good in content that spawns a lot of rares, like legion for example.

If you're not doing high rare count content than I would skip that loadout and move onto the next one.


---Fledging vs no fledging and optimal single target damage. - Yes far shot from the helmet and ascendancy do stack together, giving you 90% more damage. They're different modifiers.

Fledging is by far the best choice for pure map blasting, as it's just often 60% more damage at 70m, but it's a downgrade on bossing and something you swap off of if doing boss content early on.

For pure boss damage, it's most optimal to be in point blank range, to constantly get a double hit with your LA/ele hit - You wont get a double hit if you're far shot range, because tornado spawns near you.

This means if you're doing boss content, it's best to click point blank on your skill tree and stand in melee range vs bosses and it would be best to drop return proj for another damage support (inspiration)

Keep in mind, this does drastically change when you acquire Nimi's, as the ring make fledging optimal for both bossing and mapping, as you're able to stand in melee range and still get max far shot value.


---Steelskin vs immortal call
Steelskin is generally the better guard skill to use, but requires a high str level to use. Generally I would use immortal call until you acquire a lethal pride, which than solves all of your str issues, meaning you can swap to steelskin.


---Hyrri's Ire vs Daresso's vs Rare chest
On ele hit daresso's is roughly equal DPS to hyrri's, but doesn't provide 30% spell suppression. The chest is extremely cheap though, while hyrri's is usually 7-10d on league start. 20% MS is also very nice early on with daresso's

Hyrri's ire is the highest damage option, but can be expensive on league start. If you're playing lightning arrow over ele hit, than I would aim for this chest as the flat provides a bigger incease for this skill vs ele hit.

Rare chest is the best option but the most expensive option. Allows you to match the dps of hyrri's, while providing resist or attributes and giving equivalant evasion - Don't go for this option till you can make an all T1-T2 chest.

---Hyrri's truth vs Yoke of suffering
Yoke is the better option but also way more expensive, while hyrri's is extremely cheap and effective. Yoke is a pretty late game option, the biggest reason to swap to yoke is to help with resist.

----Which 50% auras to use? Grace - Haste - Anger - Purity of elements
Anger is only really used when you can use 3 50% auras, as the damage it provides isn't massive while playing elemental hit. Has bigger value if you're playing lightning arrow because of the flat.

In the early game you're generally going to be using Purity of elements with haste or grace on elemental hit. This depends if you want more speed vs higher evasion for defense.

Purity of elements just helps with resist and gives cheap ailment immunity, later on when you can afford an Ancestral vision, you'll swap off of Purity of elements.

You can also drop purity of elements after getting tamings, since they give a large amount of resist.

Keep in mind when you swap off of purity of elements, you will want to achieve 100 ailment avoid. You'll get 50% from ancestral vision, 30% from boot implicit and 20% from thick skin life wheel on the tree.

When entering Maps I prefer to use haste and anger and swapout one of them for grace in red maps (t11+)


---Lethal pride
You're looking for a 2 double damage or 3 DD lethal pride - My video on how to search for them efficiently - https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_kYy-s_B-ng


---How do you proc bleed for cornered pray?
The small nodes on this wheel already gives you % chance to bleed and dealing any amount of phys damage can bleed, so even lightning arrow/torando shot will bleed enemies.

For Elemental hit, you'll use a manaforged arrow setup with puncture, to gaurentee a bleed on bosses.

Once you have Headhunter, you can drop this node, just depends on the content you're doing. As it's not high value if you're doing max clear speed strats where you one tap most packs.


---Pierce vs fork/chain?
As always pierce is just the superior option when league starting, as you usually want awakened fork before swapping. Pierce is just a low investment of glove implicit or 3 passive points.

For tornado shot though, you always want to be chain or the clear feels mediocre, as you don't have AoE on arrow impact, which is what makes pierce feel good on LA/Ele hit.


---Fledging and deadeye far shot, do they stack?
Yes. The deadeye ascendancy node just has the name "far shot" but gives a different unique stat, making it stack with fledging. Giving you up to 90% more damage at max range, which is 70 units.


---Is Taming mandatory?
No. While taming is very strong and it's something you should priotize, it's not needed for the build to funcftion. It just provides a ton of increase damage and resist, making it one of the best rings you can use until mirror rings.

Death rush^7 is also a very good option for pure map blasting. But provides no benefit for bosssing.


---Good jewel stats
For elemental hit, any crit multi works because the skill is all elements. Ideally you want 2 multi on your rare jewel

Other good stats are life, atk speed, inc dmg, bow dmg.

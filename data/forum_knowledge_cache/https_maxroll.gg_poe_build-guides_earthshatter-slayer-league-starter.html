Earthshatter Slayer League Starter for Path of Exile Mirage 3.28

by cArn_
Mirage 3.28Last Updated: March 30, 2026

Advertisement

This website is supported by ads.

The Earthshatter Slayer is a very tanky, cheap and hardcore-viable league starter. Earthshatter is a very well rounded Slam skill that has good coverage and high damage on a low budget. The Slayer ascendancy provides drastically improved AoE, excellent recovery through overleech, and a high-threshold execute. This build is very strong as soon as you enter maps all the way into the endgame.

Earthshatter sends out spikes that deal heavy damage that you can then detonate with your warcries for massive overlapping explosions. These Warcries also empower your attacks, providing a big damage boost.

On top of solid damage, you have generous defensive options. Slayer has continuous leech effects which let you ignore most damage over time and provide a consistent source of healing. Plus, Slayer is well positioned to access Armor and maximum Resistances, creating a well-rounded defensive package.

This build doesn't require any Unique items to function. But the build does benefit from several common Uniques including Sinvicta's Mettle, Obscurantis or Hinekora's Sight.

Resistances

You do not need specific resistances on any item, just get the required total resistances amongst all gear combined. You need to cap your Resistances at 75% unless otherwise specified in the guide!

Leveling
Earthshatter
  • From level 1 to 12:
    • The most efficient way to play is using Spectral Throw. Try to keep enemies at just under the maximum range to deal maximum damage. You can also swap Volley Support with Faster Attacks Support on bosses for better single target.
    • Since Spectral Throw is not a skill everyone likes to use, you're completely free to keep using Ground Slam. This costs you a couple of minutes at most, so it's not that big a deal.
  • From level 12 all the way through the campaign use Sunder, a powerful slam with a lot of range.
    • Intimidating Cry doubles your single target damage for two attacks, so use it whenever it's off cooldown when fighting bosses.
    • Make sure Blood and Sand is on "blood" stance. Swapping stances is not worth it.
    • At level 24 you get Autoexertion, which means you don't have to press your warcry anymore.
  • Once you reach maps, Earthshatter becomes your main damage skill. Using this skill sends out multiple spikes that you can detonate using your Warcries for devastating explosions.
    • You can only have 3 sets of spikes placed at any given time, so use a warcry to detonate them at least every 3 attacks.

Ascendancy

How to Ascend

Learn how to find the Trials of Ascendancy and complete The Lord's Labyrinth to earn your Ascensions with our Labyrinth Guide!

  • Impact gives you a good chunk of damage from close range, additional area of effect while mapping and a lot of accuracy, enabling the Precise Technique keystone.
  • Bane of Legends is a strong damage node overall, giving you mapping and boss damage as well as immunity to Physical reflect.
  • Brutal Fervour not only prevents your Life leech recovery from stopping when you reach Full Life while doubling the duration of your Life leech effects, but also grants you an additional 10% damage reduction, making it an extremely powerful defensive node.
  • Headsman allows you to cull enemies you hit that are at 20% or less Life, resulting in a minimum of 25% overall damage increase. This value goes even higher against enemies that are not full life when you encounter them, such as Sirus in his last phase, and allows you to skip some of the hardest mechanics some bosses throw at you. Another example is The Searing Exarch, where the ball phases get harder and harder as he gets closer to dying. This ascendancy node skips the hardest part of the fight!
  • Endless Hunger gives you a good amount of attack speed, Stun immunity and makes you unaffected by Bleeding. This is mostly used in the Tainted Pact setup to prevent reflected bleeds from killing you.
  • Masterful Form is a very powerful node that allows your modifiers to Maximum Frenzy Charges to also grant you Maximum Endurance charges. It also grants you an additional Maximum Frenzy Charge. However, it is only used in the Schizophrenic Dissociation version of the build, where you drop Headsman and Bane of Legends.
Ascendancy
Schizophrenic Dissociation
Tainted Pact

Schizophrenic Dissociation from the Delirious Bloodline Ascendancy means that with the amount of recovery you have, you effectively take 40% less damage from hits at the cost of not being able to use base jewels. However, the build does not gain that much from these jewels, so it is a very strong choice.

How to Progress

After finishing the campaign, you begin interacting with the Atlas of Worlds. Learn more about how to progress your Atlas check out our Atlas progression guide! Or head over to our Boss section to learn how to defeat the Eldritch Horrors you encounter while exploring the Atlas.

  • Leveling Trees - During the campaign, you play Sunder, a hit build that benefits from nodes like Hatchet Master and Martial Experience. Getting Precise Technique enabled early with Precision aura provides you with a lot of damage and solves your accuracy.
  • After doing Merciless lab, you switch to the Early Maps tree, and continue into the Early Endgame Tree. You should have enough respec points available by the end of the campaign to not have to spend any gold for that.
  • The Super Endgame tree requires you to have your Clusters as well as the spell suppression gear, although you can start using your clusters as soon as you have them.
  • The Tainted Pact tree is used when you swap to that setup, though it is only a small tweak.
  • The Schizophrenic Dissociation tree is a more defensive option you have at your disposal once you have unlocked that ascendancy node.

Offensive Scaling

  • High Physical Damage on your axe, ideally on a Vaal Axe or Void Axe
  • Attack speed
  • #% increased (Physical or Attack) Damage
  • Exerted Attacks deal #% increased Damage
  • #% more Maximum Physical Attack Damage
  • #% increased Physical or Attack Damage with Axes

Defensive Scaling

  • Sufficient +#% to Fire, Cold and Lightning Resistance to cap your Resistances.
  • Maximum Life.
  • Armour.
  • Endurance Charges
  • +#% to all maximum Elemental Resistances
  • Fortify

Resistances

You can get Resistances wherever you want and they do not have to be on specific items. Resistances are incredibly important though. DO NOT forget about them when choosing which items to craft or buy! Make sure to check your Resistances in your Character Sheet (C, Defense Tab) whenever you switch your gear around. To learn more about defences in Path of Exile, check out our guide here!

Gear in this section is easier to craft if you obtain a Fractured base. This base 'locks' a modifier, so you can use  Essences  and other crafting methods to obtain a good item. You can search for fractured modifiers on trade by inputting "~fractured (name of modifier)" in the modifier search box. If you want to try out how to craft your final items, the Craft of Exile simulator is an amazing tool to plan them without wasting in-game currency.

Early Maps
Endgame
Super Endgame

  • In early maps, your main focus should be on getting your resistances capped and having life on every piece of gear.
  • A Sinvicta's Mettle is a good early axe that can take you all the way to red maps.
  • Otherwise, gamble for 2-handed Axes at Faustus for a decent chance to get a good Axe.
  • Don't neglect your flasks, but you don't need perfect ones early on. Try to get some way to remove corrupting blood, and get Freeze removal on your quest Quicksilver Flask from Act 1.
  • You can ignore Eater and Exarch implicits early on.

  • Once you go up in map tiers, you want to upgrade your axe. Vaal Axe and Void Axe are ideal bases, but any high level base works as long as it has high Physical Damage and as much attack speed as possible.
  • Hinekora's Sight is a relatively common unique that make it very easy to enable Precise Technique.
  • Make sure you have taken the time to roll your flask properly at this point.
  • Don't forget to anoint Hatchet Master as soon as possible to be able to respec that part of the tree.
  • Shock immunity is important to have in the endgame, so focus on getting this stat on the Abyss Jewel socketed in your Stygian Vise and your Boots by using a Screaming Essence of Torment or better.
  • Every item that can have Eater and Exarch implicits should have Eater and Exarch implicits at this point.

  • Your focus at this point is to get a Body Armour with as much Armour, Life and Physical Damage Reduction as possible, as it allows you to use the next big upgrade for the build
  • Echoes of Creation is a massive damage increase for the build, but is really only safe to use once you acquire the Super Endgame Body Armour.
  • Get your suppression gear. While Suppression is not absolutely mandatory, it makes you much tankier against spells.
  • By now, you want to have the best Axe possible. Recombinators are the best way of crafting one.
  • Endurance Charge rings and Boots are also by no means necessary, but are another big boost to your defense. These aren't as cheap or easy to acquire, so they should be your final upgrades.

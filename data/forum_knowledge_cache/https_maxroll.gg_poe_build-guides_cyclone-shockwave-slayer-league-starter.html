Cyclone Shockwave Slayer League Starter for Path of Exile Mirage 3.28

by Cptn Garbage
Mirage 3.28Last Updated: March 6, 2026

Advertisement

This website is supported by ads.

Spin to win on the Cyclone Shockwave Slayer League Starter by always staying mobile as you cut through hordes of enemies. Shockwaves from Shockwave Support create large, damaging Area of Effect pulses whenever your Cyclone hits an enemy, providing you with comfortable screen clear. To top it off, General's Cry constantly spawns copies of yourself, which then Cyclone towards the nearest targets to kill them.

This build does not use Cyclone of Tumult in favor of higher mobility and less ramp time. You may however easily switch to Tumult for its higher base damage, at the cost of lower movement speed. Simply equip The Stampede or Replica Stampede and replace Death Rush with a Rare Ring to fix resistances instead. Also replace the Cyclone in your General's Cry with Cyclone of Tumult as your clones inherit your Tumult stacks for bonus damage.

This build doesn't require any Unique items to function. But the build does benefit from several Uniques including The Magnate, Death Rush, Rumi's Concoction and Abyssus for more daring players.

Resistances

You do not need specific resistances on any item, just get the required total resistances amongst all gear combined. You need to cap your Resistances at 75% unless otherwise specified in the guide!

Skills

3.28 - Mirage
Cyclone Shockwave Slayer League Starter
Spectral Throw and Sunder
Cyclone
General's Cry
  • From level 1 to level 12 you use Spectral Throw, a boomerang-like skill, to kill packs of monsters and bosses.
    • Tip to maximize damage: Position yourself so the projectiles return point is inside the monster. This allows it to hit up to 4 times instead of the usual 2 to maximize single target damage.
  • From level 12 to 28 Sunder is the most effective choice until you can swap to Cyclone at 28.
  • Use Frostblink and Leap Slam to quickly move around
  • Reserve Precision by activating it on your Skill bar for a boost to Accuracy. Your maximum Mana should go down when done correctly, unless you are in Town.
  • Use Decoy Totem or Steelskin on tanky monsters for extra safety.
  • Level 12+: reserve Precision, Blood and Sand (Keep this in Blood Stance!), Herald of Ash (for cleanup and to enable Ignites for Fire Mastery) and Autoexertion.
  • Level 28 is when you can equip your core skill Cyclone. Cyclone causes your character to spin your Weapon around you and allows you to freely navigate in any direction while doing so.
  • Link Shockwave Support to Cyclone in order to massively boost your burst damage and area coverage. Shockwave charges up to 4 stacks, which are then used as you rapidly hit with Cyclone.
    • Important: While Cyclone can be used with any melee weapon, Cyclone supported with Shockwave cannot. This means in order to use Shockwave Support you must use a Mace or Staff.
    • If you find a really good Axe or Sword during the Campaign, it's okay to sub out Shockwave for another Support gem until you find a Mace or Staff. Though keep in mind Shockwave Support is very strong so slightly worse, but compatible weapons, should be prioritized.
  • General's Cry linked to Cyclone and Autoexertion perfectly supplements your damage output by periodically creating clones of yourself, which then use Cyclone on the nearest enemy before disappearing.
    • Important: General's Cry requires corpses to spawn your clones. While clearing there generally are enough corpses of killed enemies to draw from, automating it completely. For single target situations, you must use Desecrate once to enable their spawns.
    • There is no need to constantly cast Desecrate, as the corpses are not consumed. Make sure to recast Desecrate whenever the old corpse pile is too far away.

Ascendancy

3.28 - Mirage
Cyclone Shockwave Slayer League Starter
  • Impact gives you a lot of good stuff. The 50% increased Accuracy Rating makes it easier to satisfy the Precise Technique condition. Strike Range and AoE per kill increases the AoE of your Cyclone later on and 15% more damage is 15% more damage.
  • Bane of Legends grants a powerful generic boost in damage and makes you immune to reflected damage in Endgame Maps.
  • Brutal Fervour is the Notable that defines the Slayer Ascendancy. This causes your life leech to continue in the background for up to 20 seconds, even when at full life. This means as long as you occasionally hit an enemy, you recover your life extremely quickly once you do get hit. It also grants generic 10% reduced damage taken while leeching, which is effectively always.
  • Headsman causes any Enemy that would fall below 20% HP to die instantly, resulting in a 25% DPS increase (Why 25%? A 50% cull would half the monster's HP, which would effectively double your damage). This is especially valuable against targets that get more dangerous at low HP, such as Sirus or the Searing Exarch.

    • You can use your Faustus to respec if a new skill or gear upgrade leaves you unable to use your skills/items.

Campaign Tree

  • The +20 Int from Mana Flows allows you to use Frostblink. Alternatively you can buy a Lapis Amulet from Lily and skip this node.
  • The Fire Mastery at Burning Brutality grants a lot of extra damage against ignited enemies. Only fire damage can ignite, so make sure to run Herald of Ash for the Physical Damage as extra Fire Damage, as otherwise the invested points are purely cosmetic.
  • Precise Technique requires your Accuracy to be higher than your maximum Life to grant a massive 40% more damage. While very powerful, it's not uncommon to forget to keep up with the required Accuracy. Occasionally check your "Main Hand Accuracy Rating" in your Character Panel to make sure you're still enjoying 40% more damage.
  • The rest of the tree is straight forward as you simply pick up various sources of damage, mana sustain and life as you spin through the campaign.

Crit Swap

  • You eventually prune the right side of the Skill Tree to free up points that go towards Crit instead.
  • Here's a checklist of things you should have before the switch:
    • Level 85+
    • A high physical DPS staff with an "#% increased Critical Strike Chance" roll
    • Assassin's Mark linked to Mark On Hit Support and Lifetap Support
    • A Diamond Flask and a "#% increased Critical Strike Chance during Effect" roll on any of your flasks.
    • Optional: Abyssus. Abyssus grants a LOT of extra damage, but does make you more susceptible to Physical Damage. Use it unless you prefer to be more durable at the expense of a lot of extra damage.
      • Later on, once your damage is more than enough, you can switch the Abyssus for a Rare helmet instead. Or keep it on to watch the number go up. Both your DPS and death counter.
  • That didn't happen! from the Delirious Bloodline is an extremely powerful intermediary node as you save up for the Cluster Jewel setup. Only do this if you can get a Simulacrum carry for cheap. Replace Headsman for it.

Cluster Endgame

  • Only switch to this tree once you have the same or equivalent Cluster jewels. Overlord in particular is a very rare but powerful mod, which allows you to completely unspec the Steadfast wheel.
  • 3 Notable Physical Damage Large Clusters are worse than the shown Staff cluster but still very good.
  • IMPORTANT: This tree unallocates both Mana Cost wheels. Keep these until you have your Double Influenced Body Armour for the -20 Cost mod.
  • Use Runegraft of Treachery on the Barbarism Life Mastery

Gearing

Resistances

You can get Resistances wherever you want and they do not have to be on specific items. Resistances are incredibly important though. DO NOT forget about them when choosing which items to craft or buy! Make sure to check your Resistances in your Character Sheet (C, Defense Tab) whenever you switch your gear around. To learn more about defences in Path of Exile, check out our guide here!

Gear in this section is easier to craft if you obtain a Fractured base. This base 'locks' a modifier, so you can use  Essences  and other crafting methods to obtain a good item. You can search for fractured modifiers on trade by inputting "~fractured (name of modifier)" in the modifier search box. If you want to try out how to craft your final items, the Craft of Exile simulator is an amazing tool to plan them without wasting in-game currency.

Item Bases

It is very important to itemise your character with as much Armour as possible as it ensures the build feels comfortable to play. Prioritising the Highest level item bases for every slot sets your build up to have as high baseline defences as possible.

  • During the campaign, try to get life and Resistances on every non-weapon item. But make sure to prioritize gem links on items as well.
  • Equip a Sapphire Ring for Merveil.
  • Vendoring an Iron Ring with a red, green or blue gem gives you a normal rarity Ruby Ring, Topaz Ring or Sapphire Ring respectively.
  • Buy a white Amber Amulet or Lapis Amulet from Lily in Act 1 if you run into attribute problems.
  • Your Weapon is your most important piece. Regularly upgrade your Weapon by checking Vendors for higher tier bases (which have substantially higher base damage), set your item filter up to highlight promising bases to identify, gamble for them at the respective Vendors in town for Gold or buy them from other players.
    • Getting unlucky with bad affixes? Sell your highest white Weapon base (buy it from a Vendor) and sell it back to them together with a Blacksmith's Whetstone and a Rustic Sash. The rarity of the Rustic Sash determines how much % increased Physical Damage the weapon you get in return has. So if possible, use an Orb of Transmutation (magic) or a low level Essence (rare) on it beforehand.
  • You can use the Crafting Bench to craft any extra Resistances for 1 Orb of Transmutation. This works on normal (white) rarity items as well and is a quick way to quickly shore up your resistances.
  • Equip Two-Stone Rings after level 20, and get your Fire, Cold and Lightning Resistances as close to 75%+ as possible.

  • As you enter the Atlas of Worlds and begin mapping, your first goal is to cap your resistances and meet Attribute requirements.
  • Your first goal should be to check whether your Weapon is adequate, as it's the largest contributor to your overall damage output.
  • Prioritize getting a 6-link Body Armour with high Armour values. Try to not have any life modifiers such as +# to Maximum Life or Life Regeneration as it disables the very strong "15% increased maximum Life if there are no Life modifiers on equipped Body Armour" Life Mastery.
  • Aim for Armour on your other pieces too, but prioritize high Life, Resistances and Movement Speed on your boots.
  • Pick up an early Rumi's Concoction to boost your Block Chance
  • Here are a few other optional Uniques to look out for, though some may be expensive:
    • Carnage Heart for a good mix between Attributes, Resistances and damage. A good enough Amulet to anoint Enigmatic Defence on.
    • The Magnate for a large boost in damage. Beware that you must hit the 400 Strength requirement for The Magnate to be worth equipping.
    • Ryslatha's Coil as an alternative high damage belt.
    • Death Rush for a generically powerful boost of Damage, movement speed and damage reduction thanks to Adrenaline.

  • Lethal Pride grants a lot of Strength to help with gearing. Look for useful mods on notables but don't overinvest as your Currency is more useful in other areas at this point.
  • Use your Eldritch Embers and Eldritch Ichors on your Rares until you get decent mods for cheap upgrades.
  • Make sure to have the basic flask affixes at this point:
    • increased Armour

  • Switch to a Cluster Jewel Setup once you have similar Clusters as shown in the guide.
  • Overlord is rare but allows you to save 5 points by removing the Fortify wheel.
  • Body Armour: A high Armour double influenced Hunter-Warlord Royal Plate Stat priority for the Hunter-Warlord Chest:
    • You can apply an additional Curse
    • Socketed Attacks have -20 to Total Mana Cost
    • Attacks have +#% to Critical Strike Chance
    • High Armour Values
    • Physical damage taken as X
    • Additional Physical Damage Reduction
  • A regular Eldritch Influenced Chest with 6000+ Armour and % Additional Physical Damage Reduction is also a good choice before you craft the influenced Body Armour.
  • Use a Physical Damage or Chaos Resistance The Light of Meaning to gain more damage or help with suffix pressure, respectively.
  • Look for a Lethal Pride with as many sources of Double damage, crit multi, melee damage and other useful stats.
  • Watcher's Eye with "Impales you inflict last 2 additional Hits while using Pride". Here are other good mods to pair it with:
    • #% chance to Impale Enemies on Hit with Attacks while using Pride
    • #% chance to deal Double Damage while using Pride
    • #% increased Physical Damage while using Pride
    • #% increased Physical Damage while using Precision
    • #% increased Attack Speed while affected by Precision
    • +#% to Critical Strike Multiplier while affected by Precision
  • There are two great options for Forbidden Flame: More Than Skill and Master of Metal
  • Progenesis is expensive but very strong.
  • As your budget increases, the gearing choices open up depending on what content you plan on running. This means there is not one answer, but simply options.
  • Belt: Mageblood, Headhunter and the new Shrine belt Screams of the Desiccated
    • Mageblood is the best choice if you value unconditional movement speed and defenses as well as easier gearing.
    • Headhunter for juiced mapping. Consider substituting Brutality Support with Increased Critical Damage Support as Headhunter buffs grant a lot of extra elemental damage.
  • Gloves: An excellent Rare or Soul Ascension. Soul Ascension pairs better with Mageblood as Resistances get very tight otherwise.
  • Amulet: Focused Amulet or Stranglegasp (Check FAQ for Anoints). A 2 mod Reflected Focused Amulet easily beats Stranglegasp, though requires investment in Block Chance elsewhere. (e.g. Tattoos)
  • Helmet: A strong Rare is the recommended, balanced choice. Two other options are a corrupted Abyssus or The Gull.
    • If your goal is merely chasing DPS numbers, Abyssus is impossible to beat. Though at a certain point your DPS is more than plenty without having to give up durability for it.
    • The Gull is a great choice when running Shrine heavy strategies.
    • An excellent Rare is the most balanced option.
  • Rings: One Ring with Vulnerability on Hit. The second ring can be either a Death Rush for maximum Speed or a strong reflected Ring.
    • Careful: Vulnerability on Hit requires a source of You can apply an additional Curse from either your Anoint or Body Armour
    • Non-Mageblood setups can also opt for Replica Witchfire Brew instead
  • Boots: High movement speed Rare (either Eldritch or double influenced Tailwind+Elusive boots) or The Stampede in case you prefer Cyclone of Tumult
  • Flasks: Progenesis

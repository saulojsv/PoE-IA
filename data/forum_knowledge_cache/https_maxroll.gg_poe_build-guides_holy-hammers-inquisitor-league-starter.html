Holy Hammer Inquisitor League Starter for Path of Exile Mirage 3.28

by Strafeous
Mirage 3.28Last Updated: April 29, 2026

Advertisement

Holy Hammers is a lightning based slam skill that will be your main focus. You're able to use Holy Hammers at level 24 which means you need to use a differen't ability until then. That ability will be Holy Strike.

Holy Strike is an ability in Path of Exile that mixes melee combat with spectral weapons. You only use Holy Strike up until around your mid 30s, early 40s. After that, transition into Holy Hammers ideally around or after Cruel Labyrinth.

Holy Hammers is a lightning-based slam skill that converts 50% of your physical damage into lightning. When you slam, it also calls hammers down from the sky that cascade behind your target up to two times, dealing damage across the screen in a way that feels similar to Volcanic Fissure of Snaking. If you expend a Power Charge, that cascade doubles from two to four hammers, massively increasing the area coverage and damage.

The Inquisitor ascendancy fits the theme perfectly. Sanctuary gives you reliable Consecrated Ground while Righteous Providence provides a large amount of free critical strike chance. Together, they create a strong build and really sell the holy paladin fantasy.

If you enjoy the classic paladin fantasy of bringing divine punishment down on everything around you, this build delivers exactly that. Slam the ground and call down Holy Hammers from the sky, wiping out packs and crushing bosses with massive bursts of lightning-infused damage.

Resistances

You do not need specific resistances on any item, just get the required total resistances amongst all gear combined. You need to cap your Resistances at 75% unless otherwise specified in the guide!

Mapping
Aspirational
  • Holy Strike is as simple as it sounds: you smack the boss and holy weapons spawn and smack the mobs alongside you.
  • Thanks to Spiritual Command and Spiritual Aid, every single talent and gear mod that gives any minion buffs also apply to you. So, taking these nodes aren't just buffing your Holy Flame Totem and Holy Strike weapons, they're also buffing you.
  • It’s recommended to swap to Holy Hammers around act 6, but provided is a talent tree to level 80 if you enjoy Holy Strike and wanna stick with it as long as possible.
  • Holy Hammers is a lightning based slam attack that converts 50% of your physical damage into lightning damage.
  • Holy Hammers cascades Hammers down in a line behind the mob you slam.
  • Holy Hammers can cascade twice.
  • With the addition of Physical to Lightning Support, you convert all of your physical damage on your weapon into lightning damage.
  • When paired with Melee Physical Damage Support, this becomes a lethal combo scaling your lightning damage like crazy.
  • Autoexertion makes your war cries automatically trigger when their cooldown is available.
  • Autoexertion also reserves mana total to all of the warcries combined.
  • Most Warcries exert your next attacks, which provides a massive damage boost for slam skills. Each Warcry exerts a different number of attacks, letting you stack multiple powerful slams before needing to refresh them.
  • Gem Quality on your warcries is extremely important since it gives each of your warcries 40% cooldown reduction.
  • The Warcry tooltip displays a number that shows how many exerted attacks remain. Each time you attack, that number goes down. Once it reaches zero, the Warcry no longer exerts your attacks and needs to be cast again.
  • Aim to have a 6 link Holy Hammers by maps.
  • Adding in Blood and Sand and being in blood stance is a huge damage boost.
  • Assassin's Mark paired with Mark On Hit Support is guaranteed power charges which makes your Holy Hammers do 20% more damage and cascade an extra two times.
    • Note: The quality on Assassin's Mark grants a chance to gain a Power Charge when hitting a marked enemy. Because the mark is applied automatically through Mark On Hit Support, you can still generate Power Charges during boss fights where enemies aren’t dying.
  • Cast when Damage Taken Support paired with Molten Shell gives you a ton of EHP.
  • Once you're ready to transition to this point, you want to start imbuing every skill gem you have in hopes of landing a perfect support for it.
  • Power Charge On Critical Support or Increased Critical Damage Support are perfect for Holy Hammers.
  • The skill gems you aren't planning on imbuing can be corrupted to level 21.

Rotation

Mirage 3.28
Holy Strike Inquisitor Mirage League Starter
  • Your rotation is extremely simple: keep up Blood Rage and slam slam slam. Most of your abilities are automated and this is mostly a "position correctly and slam" build.
  • Keep Blood Rage up at all times. Blood Rage increases attack speed, gives you life leech, and gives you a chance to get a frenzy charge on kill.
  • Leap Slam to traverse around the map quickly.
  • Use Rallying Cry before slamming for buffed exerted attack damage.
  • When you hit, Assassin's Mark curses the enemy automatically thanks to Mark On Hit Support.
  • Autoexertion keeps your attacks exerted fulltime.
  • Frostblink is your "get out of trouble" button.

Tips

  • Turn on "Always attack without moving" on Holy Hammers. Without it, you constantly cancel your Holy Hammers and the build will just feel awful.
  • Use Rallying Cry when you have 5 sentinels up from Herald of Purity for the maximum Rallying Cry buff effect. This is not something you need to do unless you want to squeeze every inch of damage that you possibly can.
  • Frostblink is an instant cast and has no wind up or animation before or during the gap closing so prioritize Frostblink in sticky situations that you can't Leap Slam out of.
  • Holy Hammers gains massive damage off of expending a power charge from increasing Holy Hammers gem quality.
  • Assassin's Mark and your anoint Disciple of the Forbidden both have a chance to grant a power charge on kill.

Ascendancy

Mirage 3.28
Holy Strike Inquisitor Mirage League Starter
  • Righteous Providence is your first priority. It's a ton of free crit for close to no investment since you already have a lot of strength and intelligence from both nodes and gear you find along your journey.
  • The second ascendancy is up to you. Your choices are Inevitable Judgement or Sanctuary.
    • Inevitable Judgement allows your crits to completely ignore the mobs resistances and non crits to penetrate 10% of the resistances. It synergizes perfectly with Righteous Providence.
    • Sanctuary creates consecrated ground whenever you stop to attack, regenerating your life and reducing the effect of curses on you.
  • Pious Path Is your last ascendancy point. By this time, you should be full Armour/ES and Pious Path should constantly regen your health and energy shield.

Offensive Scaling

  • Minion damage and attack speed (for Holy Strike only)
  • High Physical Damage on your weapon
  • Cooldown Recovery rate and Warcry Recovery rate
  • Increased "Exerted attack" Damage
  • Increased Damage while leeching
  • Accuracy Rating (Need tons of accuracy)

Defensive Scaling

  • Sufficient +#% to Fire, Cold and Lightning Resistance to cap your Resistances.
  • Armour and Energy shield stacking.
  • Life and Strength on rings for life stacking
  • Energy shield % on amulet
  • Armor or energy shield on belt
  • Intelligence on rings for energy shield stacking

Resistances

You can get Resistances wherever you want and they do not have to be on specific items. Resistances are incredibly important though. DO NOT forget about them when choosing which items to craft or buy! Make sure to check your Resistances in your Character Sheet (C, Defense Tab) whenever you switch your gear around. To learn more about defences in Path of Exile, check out our guide here!

Stack as much Armour and Energy Shield as you possibly can. Armour + Energy Shield paired with Endurance Charges are huge EHP and will keep you alive through 99% of scenarios if you keep your charges capped.

Aspirational
  • Early game, take the gear you find, and don't tunnel vision on perfect modifiers on gear because 99% of the time, you won't find them nor need them.
    • If you intend to invest resources on gear, prioritize upgrading the physical damage on your Weapon as necessary.
  • Holy Strike scales off minion damage more than anything else, so a bone ring with minion attack speed is a nice bonus.
  • Before you swap to Holy Hammers, stacking pure Armour is perfectly fine.
    • A large energy shield pool is not important until you take Pious Path.
  • Strength and Intelligence stacking is great. Strength gives you life and physical damage while intelligence gives you energy shield, mana, and crit all thanks to Righteous Providence.
  • When transitioning from Holy Strike to Holy Hammers, you want to start building your up your warcry links so you can link them with Autoexertion.
  • Make sure you're actively swapping to Armour/Energy Shield bases from pure Armour bases.
  • Start getting Accuracy anywhere you can and be sure to craft "Warcry's don't exert travel skills" on a Ring, Belt, or Amulet. This is mandatory as you start adding Warcries to support Holy Hammers.
  • Before you head into maps, make sure you’re at least level 65 and take a minute to look over your gear. It’s pretty common to still be wearing pieces from level 30-40 without realizing it. Once you finish the campaign, you take a permanent −30% resistance penalty, so you’ll need to fix that before mapping. Get your Fire, Cold, and Lightning resistances capped at 75%, and try to push your Chaos resistance as close to 50% as reasonably as you can. If you ignore this step, the first pack of mobs is going to remind you why defenses matter.
  • You can get extremely good gear off of the market for 1c, like Kingsguard for instance. This includes weapons that do 500-600 dps.
  • Don't sleep on completing your Labyrinth Trials. Missing Sanctuary and Pious Path are detrimental to your survival in higher tier maps.
  • By the time you’re pushing into higher tier maps, you should already be using Echoes of Creation. This Helmet is extremely strong for slam builds because it allows Holy Hammers to benefit from multiple exerted attacks at once. The downside is that it deals 20% of your life as physical damage when you attack, so make sure you have enough Armour and Physical mitigation to survive it.
  • Ashes of the Stars is one of the best amulets for this build. The increased gem quality massively improves your warcries, effectively giving them extremely low cooldowns when combined with the rest of the setup. It also drastically improves the damage from Holy Hammers when they consume Power Charges.
  • Once your core gear is in place, begin imbuing your gems to fish for strong supports. Getting something like Power Charge On Critical Support or Increased Critical Damage Support on Holy Hammers is a significant damage boost.
  • If Echoes of Creation is dealing too much self-damage, remove one or two warcries from your Autoexertion setup. You can always add them back once you have more Armour and Physical Damage Reduction. Use a rare Helmet if you're unable to use Echoes of Creation.
  • Get “Warcries cannot exert travel skills” This mod is mandatory. Without it, your travel skills consume your exerted attacks and you will quite literally jump a few times and die.
  • Make sure all of your rare gear has Eater of Worlds and Searing Exarch implicits. Even basic implicits are a noticeable power increase and should be considered part of your mid-game gearing.
  • There's tech to replace Physical to Lightning Support by getting "30% Convert physical damage to lightning" modifier on your gloves and using the Eater implicit for the other 20%. This allows you to completely replace Physical to Lightning Support with Increased Critical Damage Support, Concentrated Effect Support or Power Charge On Critical Support.
  • At this point, you're looking for a close to if not perfectly crafted endgame staff. You're looking for over 1000 pdps.
  • Your rares should now be focused on all t1 modifiers.
  • By this point, you should have chaos resistance capped. Nightmare dungeons eat you alive without it capped.
  • You should now comfortably be able to run 5 warcries on Autoexertion.
  • Every skill you have should be level 21 with 20 quality or imbued.
  • Those lesser Eater and Exarch implicits should be grand at this point.
  • Mageblood can be substituted out by Headhunter in situations where you're killing a lot of rares in quick succession.

Summary

Mirage 3.28
Holy Strike Inquisitor Mirage League Starter
  • Holy Strike is a new ability released with the PoE 3.28 "Mirage" update. It spawns spectral weapons that attack next to you for a short time and scale off minion bonuses.
  • This League start build revolves around using Spiritual Command and Spiritual Aid to greatly increase the damage of you and your Holy Strike minions, alongside Holy Flame Totem and Herald of Purity minions.
  • At some point in the campaign, around cruel lab, you swap to Holy Hammers, a lightning based slam.
  • Holy Hammers converts 50% of your physical damage into lightning damage and you use Physical to Lightning Support to do the rest, so it turns into a completely lightning based skill that scales off of your physical damage.

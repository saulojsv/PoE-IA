Reap Ignite Elementalist League Starter for Path of Exile Mirage 3.28

by Manni
Mirage 3.28Last Updated: March 5, 2026

Advertisement

This website is supported by ads.

The Reap Ignite Elementalist excels at clearing massive hoards of enemies with a mix of explosions and ignite proliferations. Due to Elementalist's unique ability to amplify the buffs provided by Summoned Golems, you can progress smoothly at league start with a minimal investment into damage on your gear.

With Shaper of Flames any damage type contributes to ignites, making Obliteration an amazing item for both damage and explosions. The chaos explosions from Obliteration in turn also ignite enemies themselves, causing a chain reaction of explosions and ignites that annihilates any content with high density of enemies. For boss encounters, use Vaal Reap to apply a huge ignite while safely focusing on dodging their abilities.

Defensively, you can easily gear for high Armour and Energy Shield items, as you only need Resistances and a few Attributes on gear. A high chance to block and solid regeneration round out your defences and overall form a well-rounded league starter build.

The build does not need any specific gear to function and is entirely SSF-viable. However, Obliteration and Cluster Jewels unlock the true potential of the build.

Resistances

You do not need specific resistances on any item, just get the required total resistances amongst all gear combined. You need to cap your Resistances at 75% unless otherwise specified in the guide!

Endgame
  • You are required to finish the Cruel Labyrinth before being able to swap to the Reap Ignite Build. Here is how to bridge the gap:
  • Rolling Magma is available at level 1 and is your main clearing skill for the first few acts. Aim in front of enemies to try and get multiple hits from bouncing. Shooting through Flame Wall supplements your damage.
  • Dropping a Holy Flame Totem occasionally helps for clear and boss damage.
  • During Act 1, add Shield Charge and Flame Dash for mobility.
  • In Act 2, add Herald of Thunder and Herald of Ice and start using Wave of Conviction for its Exposure effect.
  • At level 28, swap the Rolling Magma for a Firestorm. Flame Wall can be discarded.
  • Proceed until you finish the Cruel Lab and use Gold to adapt the tree for Reap Ignite.
  • At level 28, swap the Rolling Magma for a Firestorm and use it for clearing and damage.
  • Flame Wall can be discarded.
  • At level 34, acquire the first ascendancy and add Summon Flame Golem, Summon Stone Golem, Summon Lightning Golem and Summon Chaos Golem.
  • Summon one of each Golem to gain their massive buffs.
  • Proceed until you finish the Cruel Lab and use Gold to adapt the tree for Reap Ignite.
  • Reap is your main skill for both clearing and single enemies. Even though ignites last for around 4-5 seconds, you still want to repeatedly cast Reap to maintain Blood Charges and to fish for a critical strike to enable Elemental Overload.
  • Vaal Reap is your button for extra single target damage. Not only does Vaal Reap hit harder than regular Reap, it also sets your maximum Blood charges from 5 to 9 for an 8 second duration, resulting in significantly higher DPS for regular Reap.
  • Arcanist Brand automatically casts Wave of Conviction (exposure), Flammability and Flame Surge to improve the single target DPS.
  • Shield Charge is your spammable movement ability, while Flame Dash is used for instant teleportation and crossing cliffs and obstacles. Makes sure these skills are set to "always attack in place" to make them easier to use.
  • Tempest Shield, Herald of Ash, Herald of Purity and Determination are your Mana Reservation skills. Make sure that enough Mana is unreserved to be able to cast Flammability.
  • Once Exposure you automate Exposure from an implicit on your Gloves, you anoint Whispers of Doom and replace the Wave of Conviction from the earlier setup with an Elemental Weakness.
  • Change the Determination to Malevolence once you reach 75% chance to Block Attacks to push damage further.

Ascendancy

How to Ascend

Learn how to find the Trials of Ascendancy and complete The Lord's Labyrinth to earn your Ascensions with our Labyrinth Guide!

  • Liege of the Primordial enables Golems to be used as consistent buff providers because Golems automatically respawn shortly after dying. Combined with the enhanced buff effect, this is one of the strongest power boosts available as a first ascendancy pick. Keep an eye on your golem count and make sure one of each of them is summoned at all times.
  • Shaper of Flames is the second ascendancy pick and enables you to play ignite with Reap, because all damage type are able to ignite now. The node also grants 100% chance to ignite and makes enemies convert 40% of their physical damage to fire, which is a massive defensive boon later in the game.
  • Mastermind of Discord lowers enemies' resistance by an additional 25% when Exposure is applied to them. At first, only enemies that you cast Wave of Conviction on will be affected by this, but later a glove implicit will automate this and apply the -25% resistance debuff consistently.
  • Heart of Destruction is the last ascendancy pick and grants a massive 60% increased Area of Effect. This not only affects Reap's area, but also the proliferation range and the size of Obliteration's explosions. When hitting a rare or boss, the area is conveniently traded for 30% more damage, helping your single target DPS.
  • For Pantheons, immediately pick Brine King and Abberoth during the campaign to help with Stun and Ignites. The most important upgrade is the immunity to freeze, which allows you to replace a flask that removes freeze for a more useful one.

How to Progress

After finishing the campaign, you begin interacting with the Atlas of Worlds. Learn more about how to progress your Atlas check out our Atlas progression guide! Or head over to our Boss section to learn how to defeat the Eldritch Horrors you encounter while exploring the Atlas.

  • Look to solve Dexterity and Strength requirements on gear. If you find yourself short you can spec into Agility, Might or Practical Application as a temporary solution.
  • Campaign Tree before Cruel Labyrinth - Before the transition into a fully ignite-focused build, you start as a mostly hit-based build using Rolling Magma and later Firestorm. You grab some Spell Damage from the Witch Start, some Cast Speed from Mental Rapidity and a Fire Mastery for 100% increased hit damage against ignited enemies to make the time before the transition as smooth as possible.
    • Make sure to have Golem Commander and a completed Normal Labyrinth by Level 34 to start using the 4 golems for their massive buffs.
  • After completing Cruel Labyrinth - Take a moment to adjust the passive tree to the 55+ Reap tree using Faustus' Gold respec option and swap to the Reap Ignite gem setup. This should easily be affordable with the gold you naturally picked up from playing, but be careful to not spend too much on gambling or trading before this transition!
  • Pre Cluster Jewel setup - Allocate placeholder damage passives such as Heart of Flame and Spiritual Aid with Redemption and Lord of the Dead. These passives are replaced once you acquire the Cluster Jewel Setup.
  • Endgame Tree changes
    • The Runegraft of the Fortress is a nice endgame addition to improve your Armour and Energy Shield values. Make sure that the reduced attribute downside does not prevent you from using your items.
    • The Runegraft of Treachery grants a lot of Reservation efficiency with practically no downside, as your only aura is Determination and enemies' Armour does not matter for an ignite build.
    • In the very endgame, you want to use The Red Nightmare to fully cap the Attack Block Chance to 75%. Replace all 10 Strength travel passives in its radius with Tattoo of the Ngamahu Firewalker to maximize the value from this upgrade.

Offensive Scaling

  • Additional Skill Levels to Physical Spells or All Spells.
    • this of course includes the Gem Level itself - Gem experience is a massive damage gain early on!
  • Gain x% of Physical Damage as Extra Fire, Cold, Lightning or Chaos Damage.
    • All Damage types contribute to the Ignite Damage and are practically equivalent.
  • +x% to (Fire) Damage Over Time Multiplier.
  • Ignites deal Damage x% faster.
  • %increased Fire, Elemental or Physical Damage.
    • Note that Spell Damage and Area Damage do NOT scale the ignite and are therefore mostly worthless.
  • x% increased Ignite Duration (not a direct single target damage increase, but a helpful stat for clearing.)
  • Increased Golem Buff Effect - scales Damage, Area of Effect, Damage over Time Multiplier and Cast Speed

Defensive Scaling

  • Sufficient +#% to Fire, Cold and Lightning Resistance to cap your Resistances.
  • Maximum Energy Shield and Armour on gear
  • Chance to block Attacks (Chance to block Spells is already capped from the tree alone.)
  • Chaos Resistance (Summon Chaos Golem provides around 45% already, but you eventually want to add more to cap it.)
  • Increased Golem Buff Effect - scales Armour, Energy Shield, Chaos Resistance and Life Regen.

Resistances

You can get Resistances wherever you want and they do not have to be on specific items. Resistances are incredibly important though. DO NOT forget about them when choosing which items to craft or buy! Make sure to check your Resistances in your Character Sheet (C, Defense Tab) whenever you switch your gear around. To learn more about defences in Path of Exile, check out our guide here!

Early priorities - key items

There are a few key upgrades that you want to prioritize as early as possible.

  • Acquire a Vaal Reap Gem. You can find it in Vaal Side Areas during the campaign or by using a Vaal Orb on regular Reap Gems with a 25% chance to turn into the vaal version. Make sure to level the Vaal Gem to a similar level as your current gem before replacing it, otherwise your DPS might drop significantly. Gem Quality is an important upgrade, but cannot be added to corrupted gems later, so acquire a 20% Quality version of Vaal Reap as soon as possible.
  • An Obliteration both adds explosions to increase your clearspeed, while also acting as decent DPS upgrade, eliminating the need to buy or craft a rare weapon. Upgrade it to a perfect roll once you can and eventually hunt for corruptions such as "increased Area of Effect", as the Spell Damage implicit does not increase your Ignite DPS.
  • A Cluster Jewel setup for Fan the Flames is a massive upgrade for two reasons. First of all, you can replace Ignite Proliferation Support with the stronger Added Fire Damage Support but more importantly, the ignites caused by explosions from Obliteration will now also spread, which in turn causes monsters to die and explode and form a chain-reaction for fast and satisfying clearing.

Cluster Jewels

  • The most important Cluster Jewel is 4 or 5 passive "Burning Damage" Medium Cluster Jewel where Fan the Flames is mandatory and the second notable can be anything from Circling Oblivion, Blowback, Brush with Death, Flow of Life, Student of Decay or Wasting Affliction.
  • The Fan the Flames Jewel is worth using alone at first, but you want to pair it with a Large Cluster and a second Medium Cluster as soon as possible.
  • The second Medium Cluster ideally has Wasting Affliction and Blowback, but any combination of the mods listed above is acceptable at first.
  • For the Large Cluster, you want an 8-passive "Minion Damage" Large Cluster Jewel for Primordial Bond. The tricky part here is to find a roll that positions Primordial Bond in the path to your two Medium Cluster slots, so you do not waste any additional passives. The easiest combinations that achieve this are Jewels with exactly two Notables on them, one being Primordial Bond and the other one being one of Renewal, Raze and Pillage or Rotten Claws.

Unique Items

There are a few unique items that are relatively easy to obtain, but are not considered key items for the build.

  • A Kikazaru is a helpful temporary item before you acquire your endgame rings. It protects you from Curses that often roll as map modifiers and bolsters your Life Regeneration greatly. Since Regeneration is your main form of sustain, this ring is very helpful in drawn out encounters.
  • The Cinderswallow Urn flask not only turns your ignites into a stylish green, but also adds some additional DPS and recovery. This item has variants for both its recovery on kill modifier, and also for its Veiled modifier. The ideal recovery modifier is "Recover 3% of Energy Shield on Kill" and the ideal Veiled modifier is "Enemies Ignited by you during Effect have Malediction". However any variant of this Flask is worth using, so you can start with a cheap one and eventually upgrade to the ideal variant later.
  • If you can get your hands on a pair of Vaal Caress early, you can use it as a pseudo-6-link before you acquire an actual 6-linked body armour. A 6-link setup is better, but a 4-link setup in Vaal Caress is definitely stronger than a 5-link, making these gloves a useful temporary item.
  • A Cloak of Flame can be used as an early, placeholder Body Armour before you acquire a well-rolled Sacred Chainmail.
  • If you can make up for the loss of Attributes and Resistances on other gear, a Crown of the Inward Eye is a helmet with similar Life and Defences to a perfect rare item without the effort of crafting or finding a rare item to buy.

Armour & Energy Shield

For your rare Body Armour, Helmet, Boots and Gloves, aim for bases with Armour and Energy Shield to be able to use Armour and Energy Shield Mastery to greatly increase the defences gained from your Body Armour.

Eldritch Implicits

You want to add Eldritch Implicit Modifiers using Lesser Eldritch Ichor and Lesser Eldritch Ember to your Body Armour, Helmet, Boots and Gloves once you have rare items that are good enough to not be replaced for a while. The desired implicits are shown on the example gear below. The most important one you should prioritize is "Apply Fire Exposure on Hit" from Lesser Eldritch Ichor on Gloves, as this allows you to remove the Wave of Conviction and instead reliably apply Exposure to every target instead.

Shield

The shield is a critical item for the build. You need it to provide most of your Block Chance against Attacks, but you also want it to grant some strong offensive stats such as Spell Levels, increased Damage and increased Critical Chance to improve the uptime of Elemental Overload, making this a tricky item to craft. Expect this to be one of the more expensive items to obtain for the build or check the FAQ for an overview on how to the craft the endgame shield yourself.

Endgame
  • During the campaign, try to get life and Resistances.
  • Acquire a Goat's Horn in Act 1 for its flat added damage
  • After level 24 and locating the recipe near the Solaris Temple 2 waypoint, craft added Fire Damage to Spells on your weapon.
  • After the transition to Ignite Reap after Cruel Lab, try to find a Sceptre with +1 to Physical Spells, %increased Fire Damage or Damage over Time Multiplier.
  • Use Boots with Movement Speed and rotate between 2 Quicksilver Flasks for better mobility.

  • As you finish the campaign and begin mapping, your first milestone should be to have capped Elemental Resistances, positive Chaos Resistance and the correct Flask types, including a Bleed-removal and Freeze-removal suffix on the Life flasks.
  • To improve your damage while progressing the Atlas, aim for a 5-link Body Armour (or Vaal Caress as explained above), +1 Levels to Physical Spells on Amulet and Shield and the Obliteration Wand.
  • Apply the cheap Amulet Anoint Arsonist for an easy upgrade.
  • Upgrade into rares with high Armour and Energy Shield to bolster your defences for high tier maps.
  • Use Lesser Eldritch Ember and Lesser Eldritch Ichor to add the desired implicits on gear.
  • Upgrade your flasks with well-rolled affixes and enchant them to automate their usage.
  • Your endgame shield with an "increased chance to Block" roll and a The Red Nightmare allow you to fully cap your chance to block.
  • Polaric Devastation (left Ring slot) and Death Rush are your endgame Ring choices for a large damage boost.
  • Your Amulet can have multiple strong damage modifiers, including extra Cold or Lightning Damage from the Shaper influence mod pool. The Whispers of Doom endgame anoint allows you to use Elemental Weakness for an additional damage upgrade.
  • The Light of Meaning (Physical or Fire variant) in the Witch Jewel socket is another endgame damage upgrade.
  • Level 4 Empower Support is another way to upgrade DPS in endgame.
  • The Incandescent Heart is another endgame damage option, especially when it is corrupted with additional gem levels for socketed gems, duration gems or AoE gems. Beware that with Incandescent Heart, you need capped Chaos Resistance, even when your Summon Chaos Golem is currently dead!

  • Single target damage is definitely the main weakness of the build. Regular pinnacles bosses can still be defeated relatively easily, but Nightmare Map Bosses and Uber Bosses require very high investment to beat comfortably.

    Here are some tips and tricks that help for boss encounters:

    • Capitalize on the fact that ignites last for a few seconds and focus more on positioning and dodging than constantly hitting the boss with Reap.
    • Armour is not very useful against a lot of bosses, so swapping in Malevolence instead of Determination can make the fights easier.
    • While Obliteration's 40% added Chaos Damage adds significant damage, a very well rolled rare weapon with 2 gem levels, %Fire damage and Damage over Time Multiplier will beat Obliteration by a wide margin when it comes to single target DPS. Explosions do not help much in most boss fights. Investing into a sceptre as described above can be a good idea if you plan on doing bosses regularly. A well rolled Grace of the Goddess can fill this role nicely too.

  • If the build seems very weak, check this list for troubleshooting

    • Do you have one of each of the 4 Golems summoned? Weapon swapping or unequipping gear can disable their gems and unsummon them, so double-check regularly.
    • Did you mix Cruelty Support and Deadly Ailments Support? These two both have great numbers, but do not work together.
    • Do you have enough unreserved Mana to cast Flammability? Even with Arcanist Brand, you have to pay the Mana Cost for it on each trigger. Use the small passives leading to Presage or use the Runegraft of Treachery so that you have more unreserved Mana than the Mana Cost of Flammability.
    • Are your enemies immune to ignite thanks to a map modifier or an Expedition remnant?

  • The build excels at farming content with a huge density of monsters, such as Expedition with Extreme Archaeology. Avoid Expedition Remnants that make enemies immune to Fire Damage or Elemental Ailments.

    Other good mechanics to farm with this build include Ritual, Legion, Keepers of the Flame, Strongboxes and Beyond.

  • Follow the steps below to craft the Shield from the Endgame gear example efficiently.

    • You start by collecting a few Titanium Spirit Shield with an itemlevel of 86 or higher.
    • Orb of Alteration and Orb of Augmentation the Shields for +1 Level to Physical Spell Skills or (76-81)% increased Chance to Block. The suffixes on the shields are irrelevant.
    • Use the Recombinator's "unpredictable method" to try and combine into a shield that has both of the mods listed above. Repeat until successful.
    • You now need to fill up the suffixes of the shield.
      • If the shield already has 2 random suffixes from the Recombinator result, just benchcraft any suffix.
      • If the item has multiple open suffixes, benchcraft any prefix and use Exalted Orb until the shield has 2 suffixes. Now benchcraft any suffix to fill the suffixes.
    • Doublecheck that the shield has an open prefix and full suffixes. Then use Crusader's Exalted Orb which guarantees 1% increased Damage per 1% Chance to Block Attack Damage.
    • Benchcraft Prefixes Cannot Be Changed and use the Harvest crafting option "Reforge a Rare item with random modifiers, including a Caster modifier" to guarantee some roll of increased Spell Critical Strike Chance.
      • Repeat the reforge step if you are not happy with the amount of Spell Crit Chance you rolled.
      • finish with a benchcraft of your choice or use an Exalted Orb if there are two open suffixes.

Summary

3.27 Keepers of the Flame
Reap Ignite Elementalist League Starter
  • Ignite Reap uses Physical Damage scaling to apply high damage ignites.
  • A wide range of near permanent buffs from Golems provide many generic stats, making League start smooth and item-independent.
  • Use Obliteration and Fan the Flames to create a chain-reaction of Explosions and Ignite Proliferation for amazing clear.
  • Scale both Life and Energy Shield for a sizable hybrid health pool that is protected by capped block chance for Attacks and Spells.

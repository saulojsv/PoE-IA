[3.26] LACERMAN 2 BUILD (Nice Clear Speed + EZ to Play + All Content + Tanky) (Lacerate Gladiator)

"
Trenmonster wrote:
Has anyone considered taking this the Champion route?

Since we're not really a bleed build, possibly switching the Belly to an explode chest for the clear, and then moving over to champion, taking unstoppable hero, fortitude for the perma fortify, then master of metal for the impale damage, and finally the last two points could be either adrenaline for more survive, or even Inspirational (DPS increase based on POB).

This would also open up some points in the tree since we can't be stunned as long as we have fortify as champ.

Also, fortify skill can be changed over to ruthless/conc effect for also a hefty DPS increase.

In theory, this would be more dps and survive. The only downside I see is losing out on the challenger charges.

Thoughts anyone?

I have thought about Champion change sometime ago. I think it's better to stay on Gladiator, but it's more of a choice, also budget. I just made a calculation on Pob. Got Master of Metal, Fortitude and First to Strike Last to Fall. Then changed Fortify with Ruthless and also some skill tree changes(Removed Red Storm and Unwavering Stance, added Versatile Stance and a life node). With Adrenaline enabled, DPS improves by %13.1. Without it, we lose %1.9 DPS. It won't be active most of the time, but will help on bosses. There's also %10 Physical Damage Reduction it gives. Though here if we had Endurance Charges, Physical Damage reduction would be around the same on bosses staying around 8-12% most of the time instead of %10 from Adrenaline not being on all the time. Armor bonuses are good though.

Problems are; we lose Endurance Charges, Maim and most importantly Challenger Charges. Wording "more" on challenger charges makes huge difference in clear speed for us. I think when we compare time spent clearing vs. time spent doing bosses, clearing wins by a huge margin. We also need attack speed from there to make auto targeting Lacerate machinegun work properly. Attack speed looks too high to naked eye, that's because every attack fires 2 shots on Lacerate and those 2 hit the same target/direction. So actual attack speed is half. It fires double, but actually attack speed is lower than Sunder version when hitting per target/direction. With Challenger Charges, auto-targeting does better, especially in heavily juiced maps/crowded places(Tested this today for the change). Also explody chests that give good life starts from 26ex at the moment(100+ life, %8+ life, free space to craft %8 life and mana) and are not plenty and still results in 180 life loss on Meta Gear compared to the Belly there. This increases Meta Gear cost a lot. And without the explosions we lose even more clear speed. These are the reasons I'm staying on Gladiator. But for sure build can be played on Champion. Just it'll be slower, will have more DPS on bosses, be tankier and more expensive.

Wakuum wrote:
Hey!

I'm new to POE (have played a lot of D3) and been using your build and I love it!

I have one question. Why should some of the gems be capped (cast when taking dmg, immortal call, etc)? I also take a lot of damage right now but I think it's because I don't have enough resistance.

Hi. Thanks for your nice words! CWDT is capped to grant frequent cast of Vulnerability and Immortal Call. Here is how it works. With each increasing level of CWDT, both damage we need to get for activation and gem levels it can support increases. At level 1 it triggers when we get 528 or more damage, but it can only support gems with level 38 or lower requirement. At level 10, it's 1354 damage and can support level 56 or lower gems. At level 20, it's 3272 damage and can support lv 70 or lower gems. When we increase CWDT level, frequency of skills supported will be cast gets lower(like getting 3272 damage happens less frequent than getting 528 damage), but it becomes able to support higher level gems. Most people/builds leave it around 1-7 for frequent cast, but there are different examples too. You might ask why we leave it at level 3 exactly. At level 3, it's cast frequently enough, also be able to support Immortal Call at lv.5 and Vulnerability at lv.7. I think it's the best point for both Immortal Call and Vulnerability. Btw you can see these levels as you hover on gems. Like when you hover your mouse on lv.3 CWDT, it says "This Gem can only Support Skill Gems requiring Level 42 or lower", and both lv.5 Immortal Call and lv.7 Vulnerability requires lv.42 so that's our limit for lv.3 CWDT.

For taking a lot of damage, your character is still a little early in game. Resistances are good but both life and dps needs to increase. You'll make the upgrades as you progress as the character is still new. I switched to Poe after playing lots of D3 as well. After the switch, I spent my first league mostly getting used to the game as Poe is a deep game, and you seem to be doing pretty good with that:)

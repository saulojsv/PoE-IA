Penance Brand of Dissipation Inquisitor League Starter for Path of Exile Mirage 3.28

by AoinoMiku
Mirage 3.28Last Updated: March 31, 2026

Advertisement

This website is supported by ads.

The Penance Brand of Dissipation Inquisitor League Starter is a powerful Bosser and quite a strong map clearer with its access to massive, screen-clearing explosions.

Penance Brand of Dissipation, even after the nerfs, stands as one of the top Brand skills in the game and more than likely still keeps its title as Number 1 this league.

The Inquisitor Ascendancy provides a lot of Power to Penance Brand of Dissipation at League Start thanks to Ascendancy Passives like Inevitable Judgement completely ignoring enemies resistances and shredding right trough them. This Ascendancy also boasts healthy amounts of Regen with 100% uptime on Consecrated Ground.

When reaching endgame, this build's damage starts scaling exponentially. The sky's the limit with good investment, and it maintains a good bulk with full Energy Shield and Chaos Inoculation.

Resistances

You do not need specific resistances on any item, just get the required total resistances amongst all gear combined. You need to cap your Resistances at 75% unless otherwise specified in the guide!

Survivability

Before swapping to Energy Shield and Chaos Inoculation this build can be a bit squishy, as it prioritizes damage scaling on it's earlier setups. It is still more than tanky enough for a Ranged Caster Build since you can easily keep your distant most of the time.
Enemies can't kill you if you kill them first.

  • Assassin's Mark one of the few curses that are effective with Inevitable Judgement.

Make sure to always have a replacement Skill Gem ready to go before you Slam your main Skill Gem as certain supports may make it unusable for your current build.

  • Righteous Providence facilitates getting 100% Critical Strike Chance, even quite early in your journey, by just getting attributes you already want to get.
  • Inevitable Judgement is the main reason to League Start with Inquisitor. This is a powerhouse of an Ascendancy Passive that increases your damage significantly.

    Lastly, while in Consecrated Ground , your Life regen also applies to Energy Shield, which is incredibly good for the early hybrid setups before you go Chaos Inoculation.

How to Progress

After finishing the campaign, you begin interacting with the Atlas of Worlds. Learn more about how to progress your Atlas check out our Atlas progression guide! Or head over to our Boss section to learn how to defeat the Eldritch Horrors you encounter while exploring the Atlas.

  • Look to solve Intelligence, Dexterity and Strength requirements on gear. If you find yourself short, you can spec into Attribute Notables as a temporary solution.
    • You can use your Faustus to respec if a new skill or gear upgrade leaves you unable to use your skills/items.

Campaign

  • You start your journey with Rolling Magma, since it is the best out of all the initial skills you are offered.
  • Storm Brand becomes available at level 12 and is the skill you use until you acquire Penance Brand. Make sure to swap to it when you enter Act 2.
  • Penance Brand is obtained at the end of Act 3 and you start using it in Act 4, as it provides much better clear.
  • Swap to Penance Brand of Dissipation as soon as possible, ideally before early maps.
    • Make sure to always carry extra Penance Brand gems when you go into Labs to be able to gamble them at the end and potentially get a Penance Brand of Dissipation early for free.
  • Remember to use the crafting bench to fill any free Affix slots you may have on your gear, as some extra Life and Resist rolls can go a long way.

Early Maps

  • Once you have finished the campaign, try to grab yourself a 5/6-link Chest Piece, Penance Brand of Dissipation and a good Wand or Scepter with +1 All skills and other decent stats like Spell Damage, Spell Critical Strike Chance and Cast Speed.
  • It is fine to keep Penance Brand for a bit until you manage to grab enough currency to swap.
    • Make sure to do a lot of Chaos Recipes, as it not only helps for getting enough initial currency but it is also one of the best ways to make currency until you start getting into red maps.

Endgame

  • Make sure to prepare all the gear you need before making the swap to Chaos Inoculation, as without decent energy shield gear you will just fall over when ever an enemy simply looks your way.
    • You don't need the best of the best gear to do this swap, but a good rule of thumb is to not swap until you can afford gear where all the energy shield affixes are at the very least Tier 3, ideally Tier 2.

Resistances

You can get Resistances wherever you want and they do not have to be on specific items. Resistances are incredibly important though. DO NOT forget about them when choosing which items to craft or buy! Make sure to check your Resistances in your Character Sheet (C, Defense Tab) whenever you switch your gear around. To learn more about defences in Path of Exile, check out our guide here!

Gear in this section is easier to craft if you obtain a Fractured base. This base 'locks' a modifier, so you can use  Essences and other crafting methods to obtain a good item. You can search for fractured modifiers on trade by inputting "~fractured (name of modifier)" in the modifier search box. If you want to try out how to craft your final items, the Craft of Exile simulator is an amazing tool to plan them without wasting in-game currency.

Item Bases

Throughout your Atlas progression, focus on wearing hybrid Armour and Energy Shield bases. The Armour reduces some of the physical damage you are taking while the Energy Shield provides some additional Effective Health Points, taking full advantage of your Pious Path Ascendancy passive to double the amount of Effective Health Points you regen per second. These hybrid bases are also really great since you can easily roll Blue and Red sockets, which are 90% of your skills and support gems.

Once you reach a point where you are ready to swap to a Chaos Inoculation setup, you want strictly Energy Shield bases, ideally only the top of the crop, with the exception of your Helmet, where you want a Archdemon Crown for the +2 to your Penance Brand of Dissipation.

There are two ways to obtain this Crown:

  • The easiest and probably best way, just buy it. Unless you are crafting multiple for profit, it is generally not worth to try and craft it.
  • Craft it yourself. If you are playing Solo Self-found or just want to learn how the craft is done, check the Endgame and Giga Setups just below for a breakdown on how to craft each of the Crowns.

Explosions and Clear

The clear in this build is improved in two ways: increasing Brand Attachment Range and Explosions. Brand Attachment Range is pretty straightforward, just get as much of this stat as you can without major sacrifices.
Explosions, however, are achieved in a couple of different ways on each setup.

  • On the Early Maps Setup, you sadly do not have any easy and cheap way to access explosions and so rely solely on Brand Attachment Range.
  • On the Midgame Setup, your explosions come from Inpulsa's Broken Heart. Inpulsa's Broken Heart explosions have the requirement of the enemies being Shocked, which is not really a problem since most of your damage is Lightning. However, Inpulsa's Broken Heart explosions themselves can not apply Shock, which prohibits the explosions from detonating in a massive chain. To help to remedy this issue, use the Searing Exarch implicit "Shocks you inflict spread to other Enemies within # meters" on your Gloves. This single modifier chains your Shocks across mob packs, allowing your Inpulsa's Broken Heart explosions to chain right behind them.
  • On the Endgame Setup, your explosions come from the Foulborn mod on Foulborn Berek's Respite. It is the same exact mod found in Inpulsa's Broken Heart, so you have to do the same trick as in the Midgame Setup.
  • On the Giga Setup, your explosions come from the Synthesis Implicit "Enemies you kill have a 15% chance to explode, dealing a tenth of their maximum life as physical damage" on your Weapon, either a Wand or Scepter.
    This can be quite expensive, but it frees up other slots to gain a lot of more power and defenses on build.

  • Make sure to grab and save some low item level Wands while in Act 1. They have a good chance of giving you +1 Level to all Spell Gems of a certain Elemental type and can easily be a wand you use all the way into Maps.
  • During the campaign, try to get Life and Resistances on every non-weapon item, but make sure to prioritize gem links on items as well.
  • Equip a Sapphire Ring for Merveil.
  • Using a Citrine Amulet helps a lot with meeting attribute requirements on gems and gear.
  • Try to get your Elemental Resistances as close to 75% as you can; remember to use the Crafting Bench in your Hideout as needed.
  • If you need extra Strength or Dexterity, you can use the Crafting Bench for 1 Orb of Augmentation to add what you need.
  • Try to do your first two Labs as soon as you can, as that boosts your damage tremendously. The third Lab can wait until you are able to do the 4th as well.
  • Prioritize getting a 6-link Body Armor, ideally without a Life roll, so you can benefit from the 15% increase from the Life Mastery.
  • Also try to get a decent elemental Wand or Scepter with Cast Speed, as that is the main way to increase your damage right now.
  • The rest of your gear is mostly there to give you Life rolls. Cap your Resists and meet your Attribute requirements.
  • March of the Legion, together with Eldritch Battery, provide you with an additional potent offensive aura in Hatred.
    • Hatred costs 700 Energy shield to cast. If you don't happen to have enough Energy Shield to cast it, swap Increased Duration Support for Inspiration Support for the 34% less Mana Cost it provides to any linked gem.
  • Clusters increase your damage significantly and give you access to a bunch of jewel slots. Prioritize % Life, Cast Speed and Critical Strike Multiplier. You can also use these jewels to fix some Block you may be missing or more reservation efficiency if you need.

In this setup, you run a number of Uniques:

  • The Red Nightmare, together with Tattoo of the Ngamahu Firewalker on the surrounding Strength nodes, provides a lot of Block Chance.
  • Inpulsa's Broken Heart for the explosions.
    • Make sure to have Searing Exarch implicit "Shocks you inflict spread to other Enemies within # meters" on our gloves.
  • Prism Guardian to be able to reserve more aura/support gems and enable Pain Attunement.
  • Crown of Thorns to gain access to Pain Attunement without having to spend multiple points to reach it.
    • This unique is extremely cheap and plentiful. Try to grab one with a good corruption like % life, +2 to Level of Socketed AoE Gems, +2 to Level of Socketed Aura Gems, +1 Power Charges or even Socketed Skill Gems get a 90% Cost & Reservation Multiplier if you wanna try to squeeze in Vitality into the build.
  • The Taming provides a great generic multiplier and makes capping resists extremely easy.
  • The Magnate is a practically free 10% damage multi, since you already want to get strength in this build, and some nice resistances.
  • Rumi's Concoction helps to round out Block Chance.
  • On this setup, take Chaos Inoculation and fully swap to Energy Shield.
    • You need to do this swap all at once so be sure to prepare your gear beforehand. Be sure to use the best energy shield base and have at minimum to Tier 3 and Tier 2 Energy Shield Mod rolls.
  • Foulborn Berek's Respite allows you to keep Inpulsa's Broken Heart's explode while freeing the chest piece and allowing you to go full energy shield with Chaos Inoculation.
  • Replica Dragonfang's Flight gives 3 extra ranks to Penance Brand of Dissipation and also a good amount of reservation efficiency.
  • If you wanna be extra defensive, you can even swap The Magnate for a Double Influenced Crystal Belt like this one Crystal Belt without losing much damage.
  • Forbidden Flame and Forbidden Flesh with Unwavering Crusade are a big damage spike, as this Ascendancy passive gives you Hatred, Wrath and Anger.
  • Watcher's Eye is a really easy way to get power into the build since you are affected by all the elemental offensive Auras mentioned above.
  • Atziri's Promise and Taste of Hate are used for a bit of extra damage.
  • Cinderswallow Urn is incredibly strong for this build, provided you get the right mods, those being "Recover 3% energy shield on kill" and "Enemies ignited by you have Malediction".

Helmet Craft

  1. Get an item level 84 Archdemon Crown.
  2. Apply a Shaper's Exalted Orb on it.
  3. Awakener's Orb a random Elder influenced helmet into it. (this step can be bypassed at this stage but it is mandatory if you want to eventually upgrade to something like the helmet in the Giga Setup)
  4. Use Essence of Horror on it until you hit "Socketed Gems are Supported by Level # Hypothermia, #% increased Effect of Cold Ailments".
    • There are other ways to reach this spot but this is the simpler one.
  5. Make sure your item has 1 open suffix and at least 1 open prefix.
  6. Craft "Suffixes can not be changed".
  7. Reforge Crit on the Horticrafting Station.
    • This is a 1/6 to hit "Socketed Spells have +3% to Critical Strike Chance". 1/3 if you only have Shaper Influence.
  8. If you miss, try to get lucky with an Orb of Annulment to try Step 6+7 again and avoid having to go back to Step 4.
  9. On success, you can simply craft "Suffixes can not be changed" and use a Veiled Chaos Orb.
    • If you have a lot of extra Currency, you can ignore the next steps and already try to make a helmet similar to the one in the Giga version.
  10. If the item has an open Prefix craft "+1 to maximum number of Raised Zombies, +1 to maximum number of Skeletons, Minions have (8-10)% increased maximum Life", to increased the odds of "(8-10)% increased Area of Effect, +2 to Level of Socketed AoE Gems" showing up, and unveil it.
  11. Repeat the last two steps until you do get "(8-10)% increased Area of Effect, +2 to Level of Socketed AoE Gems".
  • With the introduction of Mageblood, the build becomes way tankier, faster and gains a bit extra damage.
    • Remember to also invest in a new set of Flasks, as that is where Mageblood's Power comes from.
  • Kalandra's Touch is only here to reflect your other ring. You can just get a similar rare ring if it happens to be cheaper than Kalandra's Touch.
  • Impossible Escape, together with Elegant Hubris, allows you to cap Block with less points while potentially providing a lot of damage as well.
    • Your priority is getting two "12% chance to block attack damage" nodes.
  • Upgrade your Rares, specially your Helmet. It does not have to have lvl 25 gems or even lvl 20, but try to get one with all the stats listed.

Passive Tree Progression

PoE 3.28
Arma Crema Scion Leveling Build
  • There are 2 Arma Crema Scion Passive Tree Variants:
    • Both Variants prioritise Attack Speed and Damage by allocating, they start the same but diverge after 63 points::
      • The Explosive Impact Fire Mastery at 34 Passive Points
      • All Templar Area Attack Speed Notables at 43 Passive Points
      • Elemental Overload at 52 Passive Points
      • Practical Application, Heart and Soul, Fire Walker at 61 Passive Points
      • Purity of Flesh & Skills Cost Life instead of 15% of Mana Cost Mastery at 63 Passive Points
    • Both Variants progress through the campaign at a similar speed. Your main deciding factor is whether you want to progress with Shield Charge or Leap Slam, a decision you make in Act 1.
  • Shield Charge:
    • Remains in the Witch & Templar Area, concluding with Deep Thoughts, Cruel Preparation, Heart of Flame & Righteous Army.
  • Leap Slam:
    • Progresses to the Shadow Area, concluding with Dark Arts, Coordination & Attack and Cast Speed for greater Mobility.
  • Notes & Considerations:
    • Consider allocating Dreamer and the 12% increased Mana Reservation Efficiency of Skills Mastery after equipping your first Aura Gem at Level 24 for easier Mana Sustain.
    • Consider allocating Surge of Vigour and the Regenerate 50 Life per Second Recovery Mastery to assist with sustaining Blood Rage
    • Important: Scions do not have access to Combustion, thus you use Sniper's Mark and rush Ash, Frost and Storm for the Hits have 15% chance to treat Enemy Monster Elemental Resistance values as inverted Elemental Mastery, both of which are used for the rest of the run. This leads to the following considerations only if you want to progress with a traditional Monster Resistance Reduction build:
      • Equipping Flammability, Removing Sniper's Mark and unallocating the Inversion Mastery at Level 24.
      • Completing the Twilight Strand Quest in Act 6 and:
        • Swapping Added Lightning Damage for Combustion.
        • Swapping Elemental Proliferation for Ignite Proliferation.

Playstyle

Armageddon Brand is your primary clearing ability. Cremation is your primary single target ability.

Haste is used during zone progression and is swapped to Anger on Bosses. For even more single target damage, enemies are debuffed with Wave of Conviction, Flammability & Summon Skitterbots.

What is Muling?

In Path of Exile, "Muling" or creating a "Mule", simply means creating a second character or more, to acquire resources that improve the main characters progression.

Typical Resources of interest are:

  • Skill Gems or Support Gems
  • Life or Mana Flasks
  • Specific Items
  • Linked Items
  • & Currency

Why Mule?

There are two main benefits to muling:

  • Gaining access to resources before they are normally available
  • & Consistency

In this run the Witch Mule provides the following benefits:

  • Immediately acquiring a Driftwood Wand, Rolling Magma & Arcane Surge Support at Level 2, saving 3 Scrolls of Wisdom.
  • Acquiring 3 Extra Scrolls of Wisdom or more, to help purchase and identify items.
  • Acquiring 2 Extra Small Life Flasks to guarantee a Medium Life Flask, significantly improving survivability.
  • Acquiring an Extra Small Mana Flask, improving Mana Sustain in Act 1.
  • Providing a 2nd set of Loot from Hillock, potentially providing Linked Items, Movement Speed Boots, or Unidentified Magic or Rare Items to sell for Transmutation Shards.
  • Providing a 2nd Vendor Check, potentially providing Linked Items or Movement Speed Boots.
  • A Fireball Gem to vendor with an Iron Ring, to create a Sapphire Ring; improving survivability in High Cold Damage zones such as Act 3 Docks or versus unique enemies such as Daresso or Malachai.

For a 1m10s to 1m30s investment, the benefit is clearly worth it.

Goat Horns, Weapon Swapping & Playstyle

To maximise progression speed, this build uses Leap Slam to traverse through zones quickly. However, until the Level 24 Crafts, Axes & Swords offer no damage benefits.

Thus, from Level 5 until Level 24, equip 2 Goat's Horns in your second weapon set. The Flat Fire damage to Spells in combination with Rolling Magma's high Damage Effectiveness, significantly boosts your single target damage.

Versus Magic, Rare & Unique Monsters:

  • Use your Goat's Horns.
  • Use Flame Wall if necessary.

Versus Everything Else:

  • Use your Leap Slam Weapons.
  • Consider casting Flame Wall if it avoids weapon swapping or an extra cast.

The Level 24 Fire Crafts Breakpoint

Continuing onward, at Level 24 it's imperative to craft Flat Fire Damage to Spells on your Leap Slam Weapons. Doing so significantly boosts your damage and forgoes the need to Weapon Swap. Consider vendoring your Goat's Horns for extra currency if necessary.

Rolling Magma Double Bounce

Before progressing further Rolling Magma's Bounce mechanic needs to be understood.

Simply stated:

  • Rolling Magma Bounces multiple times per cast.
  • Rolling Magma only deals damage when the projectile hits the ground (AKA the Bounce).

In practice, this means aiming slightly in front of the target, to potentially hit an enemy twice with a single Rolling Magma.

Act-by-Act Progression Tips

Act 1

  • In the Submerged Passageway, place a Portal at the Bridge, to more quickly enter Flooded Depths Later.

Act 2

  • In the Broken Bridge, search for and defeat the 3 Exiles beside the waypoint. Keep an eye out for item upgrades, jewellery, and gear to vendor.
  • There are many Breaches in Act 2. The Breach in Riverways is often found on the way and can be a great source of extra experience and items while progressing.

Act 3

  • Solaris 2
    • Important: Acquire the Fire Damage Craft in the Room beside Lady Dialla
  • In Docks you can find a Unique enemy that drops a guaranteed Essence. They only drop Essences the first time you click them; so consider resetting the zone until you spawn an Essence you desire.

Act 4

  • Get Level 32 before entering Kaom's Stronghold to avoid severe XP Penalties.

Act 5

  • The Chamber of Innocence has many guaranteed Magic Monster packs. It's strongly recommended to go out of your way to farm them.
  • Before entering the Reliquary to complete the Passive Skill Point quest, place a portal outside of the Cathedral Rooftop entrance, to progress faster.

Act 6

  • In the Beacon, while progressing the fuel pylons, consider placing a Safety Portal as the enemies that spawn can occasionally be very dangerous.

Act 7

  • After defeating Gruest, and progressing forward to the Causeway; consider placing a portal near the Dread Thicket entrance if you see it, to progress faster.
  • Dread Thicket
    • Acquire the T2 Fire Damage Craft near Dreadstone.

Act 8

  • Upon returning to the Harbour Bridge to complete the act, reset the zone by Ctrl + Clicking the entrance for more experience along the way.

Act 9

  • Consider farming Blood Aqueduct for more experience and gear before progressing to maps.
  • Consider rolling a Bleed Removal Flask and using Grace in the Belly of the Beast.

Act 10

  • Upon entering the Ravaged Square, place a portal near the entrance, before travelling to the Ravaged Square waypoint. Then take the waypoint back to town, and go through your portal and head south, in search of The Control Blocks to defeat Vilenta.

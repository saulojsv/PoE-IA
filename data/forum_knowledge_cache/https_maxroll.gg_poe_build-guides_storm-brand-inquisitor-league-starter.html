Storm Brand Inquisitor League Starter for Path of Exile Mirage 3.28

by Velyna
Mirage 3.28Last Updated: May 10, 2026

Advertisement

This website is supported by ads.

The Strom Brand Inquisitor uses critical strikes and elemental damage to vanquish foes, while consecrating the ground he walks on to restore himself. This Storm Brand Inquisitor League Starter build smoothly scales from Act 1 through to Endgame. Your Critical Strikes ignoring enemy resistances makes it easier to deal with bosses, and the curse protection from you Consecrated ground means you can run maps which would otherwise be dangerous.

Storm Brand attaches to an enemy, periodically damaging both the attached and nearby monsters for a duration. If no enemies are nearby or the attached foe dies, brands sit on the ground for a secondary duration attempting to reattach. Cast speed increases both placement speed and damage frequency. By default 3 brands can be placed but only one may be attached to a target. Both limits can be raised using the passive tree.

During the Campaign and Early Mapping, you wear hybrid armour/energy shield rare items to provide Physical Mitigation without requiring too much Strength. When you acquire Ghostwrithe, you begin using Energy Blade and Spellblade Support to scale damage from life. Continue stacking life and life recovery, swapping to Blood Magic when you can sustain the 10% life cost from Rathpith Globe.

Resistances

You do not need specific resistances on any item, just get the required total resistances amongst all gear combined. You need to cap your Resistances at 75% unless otherwise specified in the guide!

Energy Blade
  • Rolling Magma is your main damage dealer until level 12, supported by Holy Flame Totem and Flame Wall.
  • Cast Rolling Magma near your feet, allowing it to hit enemies multiple times while bouncing forward. Combustion Support support is available early to support either Rolling Magma or Holy Flame Totem.
  • Flame Wall is a lingering ground AoE which damages enemies and buffs projectiles you fire through it. This is useful for clearing packs or dropping on bosses. Holy Flame Totem is also lingering damage but in totem form, can be dropped alongside Flame Wall to burst down bosses. The totems also create consecrated ground in a small radius around themselves, providing you life regeneration.
  • Storm Brand is offered as a quest reward near the end of Act 1. Added Lightning Damage Support and Arcane Surge Support can be added immediately. Brands are placed on the ground and attach to nearby enemies. By default, 3 brands can exist but only one can be attached to each enemy. Runebinder keystone allows an additional brand on enemies but prevents use of Holy Flame Totem, take it whenever brands feel comfortable alone.
  • Storm Brand damages not only what it attaches to but surrounding monsters as well, if the attached monster dies before the brand expires it then attaches to a new target.
  • Drop a couple brands in a pack and let them work, move and drop some in a different pack, repeat. Use Brand Recall to summon all existing brands to your position for a large pulse of damage.
  • Raise Spectre is used with a Perfect Spirit of Fortune which provides Lightning Damage is Lucky and the Wrath aura.
  • Purchase the Perfect Spirit of Fortune corpse or find it in Ritual. Place the corpse on the ground and click on it with Raise Spectre to summon it as a spectre.
  • Spectres are attached to the socketed skill gem, do not remove the gem outside town or they are lost.
  • Companionship Support causes this spectre to absorb some of the damage from hits taken.
  • Energy Blade consumes half of energy shield to overwrite the equipped mainhand weapon with a spectral blade whose damage increases with energy shield consumed. This damage is then applied to spells by way of Spellblade Support support. Ghostwrithe prevents energy shield from being reduced when creating the sword

How to Ascend

Learn how to find the Trials of Ascendancy and complete The Lord's Labyrinth to earn your Ascensions with our Labyrinth Guide!

  • Righteous Providence - You need to take Righteous Providence to path to Inevitable Judgement, in addition it provides the build with critical strike chance as you need 200 int and 300 str to use all of your gems and items. Since Righteous Providence takes the lower of the two this results in 200% increased critical strike chance.
  • Inevitable Judgement - With this node allocated Inquisitors can ignore elemental resistance completely. Things like Conductivity and Lightning Penetration Support lose value, because the monster's resistances are treated as zero by your critical strikes.
  • Sanctuary - Provides Consecrated Ground while stationary and increases its effect. Consecrated ground grants 6% life regeneration per second and 50% reduced effect of curses on you by default.
  • Pious Path - Consecrated Ground now also grants 6% energy shield regeneration per second. The effect also remains for 4 seconds while not stationary. This means casting once every 4 seconds grants a permanent buff.
  • Instruments of Virtue - When transitioning to the full Blood Magic setup Inevitable Judgement should be dropped for Instruments of Virtue. Replace Increased Critical Strikes Support with Lightning Penetration Support or Invert the Rules Support support when making this change.

How to Progress

After finishing the campaign, you begin interacting with the Atlas of Worlds. Learn more about how to progress your Atlas check out our Atlas progression guide! Or head over to our Boss section to learn how to defeat the Eldritch Horrors you encounter while exploring the Atlas.

  • Look to solve Intelligence, Dexterity and Strength requirements on gear. If you find yourself short you can spec into Attribute Notables as a temporary solutions.
    • You can use your Faustus to respec if a new skill or gear upgrade leaves you unable to use your skills/items.
  • Campaign Tree - Grab life, cast speed, spell and elemental damage, and crit. Avoid penetration and resistance lowering effects especially after allocating Inevitable Judgement. Towards the end of campaign begin taking block nodes on the tree. Pain Attunement has no value until Petrified Blood is used.
  • Mid Game - Place more points in block and power charge scaling. Cluster jewels provide substantial damage and defense in this stage. Doryani's Lesson plus Stormrider on a Large Cluster Jewel gives leech and power charges while Enduring Composure on a Small Cluster Jewel gives endurance charges. Before the large cluster leech can come from The Highwayman or an eldritch implicit on gloves.
  • End Game - Sustaining Rathpith Globe determines when you should allocate Blood Magic. Spells cost 10% of max life in addition to their normal cost, substantially more than that must be recovered per second to ensure comfort, especially with Petrified Blood and Tempest Shield reserving over half your life.

Resistances

You can get Resistances wherever you want and they do not have to be on specific items. Resistances are incredibly important though. DO NOT forget about them when choosing which items to craft or buy! Make sure to check your Resistances in your Character Sheet (C, Defense Tab) whenever you switch your gear around. To learn more about defences in Path of Exile, check out our guide here!

Gear in this section is easier to craft if you obtain a Fractured base. This base 'locks' a modifier, so you can use  Essences  and other crafting methods to obtain a good item. You can search for fractured modifiers on trade by inputting "~fractured (name of modifier)" in the modifier search box. If you want to try out how to craft your final items, the Craft of Exile simulator is an amazing tool to plan them without wasting in-game currency.

Life Stacking

This build scales various other stats by stacking life and life sustain. Energy Shield is gained from Ghostwrithe, enabling the use of Energy Blade and Spellblade Support. Rathpith Globe provides spell damage and crit but makes spells cost an additional 10% of your maximum life. High life regen combined with increased maximum fire resistance enables Righteous Fire.

  • The Foulborn version of Ghostwrithe with "Your Maximum Energy Shield is Equal to 40% of your Maximum Life" is required for the set up above.

Mid Game

For gloves and helmet, get high life and % increased life regeneration rate on bases with some armour, ideally pure armour. Don’t overspend on weapons because they become irrelevant when you start using Energy Blade. Armour/Energy Shield shields offer a nice mix of damage and defense, look for high life with spell crit and block chance.

A Stygian Vise belt is important because it can be socketed with an Hypnotic Eye Jewel providing +# life and +44% chance to avoid being shocked.

Craft boots using Deafening Essence of Sorrow to reach 100% Shock Avoidance. This matters when you equip Stormshroud but is not important until then. Tempest Shield grants you shock immunity before you craft the boots but does not enable Stormshroud.

Any 6L Armour/Energy Shield chest is fine until Ghostwrithe, having less resistance here makes the swap easier.

Call of the Brotherhood provides defensive Chill and Freeze by converting part of the damage.

Endgame

Ghostwrithe sets maximum energy shield to 40% of life, it cannot be altered otherwise. Combined with high life this means Energy Blade provides big weapon without removing anything, and this weapon damage can be applied to spells with Spellblade Support support.

Blood Magic removes all mana, making skills cost and reserve life instead. This means Tempest Shield and Petrified Blood reserve 60% of total life so swapping early is not recommended.

Stack life and regeneration until certain the downsides are sustainable, then equip Rathpith Globe and allocate Blood Magic. You should be regenerating/leeching substantially more than 10% of life per second to sustain this.

The Red Dream provides massive life when socketed in the marauder area, especially combined with Tattoo of the Ngamahu Firewalker.

Berek's Respite replaces Call of the Brotherhood and causes enemies to explode, very nice for high density maps.

Summary

Storm Brand Inquisitor League Starter
  • Storm Brand attaches to an enemy, periodically damaging it and any surrounding targets.
  • Runebinder allows 2 brands on an enemy, passives and masteries raise the overall 3 brand limit.

Storm Rain Deadeye League Starter for Path of Exile Mirage 3.28

by havoc616
Mirage 3.28Last Updated: March 31, 2026

Advertisement

This website is supported by ads.

This Storm Rain Deadeye League Starter Build Guide teaches you the most efficient way to level your character and acquire your first 4 Voidstones.

Storm Rain is a payload skill that fires arrows into the sky, which land on the ground and release beams between each other for tons of single target damage.

During the campaign you level with Rain of Arrows or Lightning Arrow, and swap to Storm Rain after acquiring Endless Munitions from the Merciless Lab.

During the campaign you survive with Life, Evasion and strong positioning; then during Atlas Progression you upgrade your survivability with even more Evasion, Energy Shield via Hybrid Gear, Ghost Dance for burst damage protection, Spell Suppression, and Cast when Damage Taken + Immortal Call.

This build doesn't require any Unique items to function. But does benefit from Uniques such as Hyrri's Truth, Hyrri's Ire, The Taming or Death Rush.

Resistances

You do not need specific resistances on any item, just get the required total resistances amongst all gear combined. You need to cap your Resistances at 75% unless otherwise specified in the guide!

Mobility
  • Storm Rain is both your primary clear and single target ability.
    • For clear: attack into each pack and run through them with a Quartz Flask, Freedom of Movement, or any other source of Phasing.
    • For single target: continuously attack the target with Storm Rain to create a damaging area.
  • Supplemental Damage is provided by Manaforged Arrows Support, which triggers a multitude of skills:
    • Frenzy to generate Frenzy Charges
    • Ensnaring Arrow which increases the damage Enemies take
    • Tornado to deal massive single target damage
    • Storm Rain which deals damage and generates Power Charges
  • Sniper's Mark + Mark On Hit significantly improves your single target damage by increasing the damage enemies take and splitting your projectiles.
  • Anger is used to increase your damage, by providing an efficient source of flat elemental damage to attacks.
  • Grace improves your survivability, by significantly improving your Evasion rating.
  • Optionally, Haste can be used instead of Grace or Anger for increased mobility.
  • A low level Precision is used to cap your hit chance and to provide a moderate but meaningful amount of crit chance.
  • While not explicitly an aura, Cast when Damage Taken + Immortal Call is used to increase your survivability against burst damage.
  • Flame Dash, Blink Arrow and running are your primary mobility methods.
  • To travel large distances quickly: use Blink Arrow and then Flame Dash.
  • Optionally: in your second weapon set you can equip Shield Charge + Momentum + Faster Attacks to travel even quicker. However, it can be quite cumbersome and tedious to use without practice.

  • Gathering Winds is the de-facto choice as your first ascendancy node to increase your action speed; resulting in significantly improved mobility, clear speed and single target damage after Normal Lab.

  • Focal Point is taken in Uber Lab to increase your single target damage.

How to Progress

After finishing the campaign, you begin interacting with the Atlas of Worlds. Learn more about how to progress your Atlas check out our Atlas progression guide! Or head over to our Boss section to learn how to defeat the Eldritch Horrors you encounter while exploring the Atlas.

  • Look to solve Intelligence, Dexterity and Strength requirements on gear. If you find yourself short you can spec into Attribute Notables as a temporary solutions.
    • You can use your Faustus to respec if a new skill or gear upgrade leaves you unable to use your skills/items.
  • Campaign: Click through the campaign embed to follow the recommended passive tree progression. For even more guidance, refer to the Rain of Arrows Deadeye Leveling Build Guide.
    • Note: The build swaps from Non-Crit to Crit during the late campaign or early atlas progression, to maximise DPS during the T16 Map Section.
  • 2 Stone: You should be fully Crit Swapped around the T16 Map Section. Ensure you've allocated Entrench and the Chance to Suppress Spell Damage is Lucky mastery for additional survivability before fighting Pinnacles. Aim for a combined total of 3K+ Life & Energy Shield and 1Million+ DPS for a smooth experience.
  • 4 Stone: Your primary passive tree goal during 4 Stone progression is to cap your Spell Suppression. Capped Spell Suppression significantly improves your survivability versus the final 2 Pinnacle Bosses: Maven & Uber Elder. Additionally, aim for a combined total of 3.6K to 4K+ Life & Energy Shield and 2Million to 3Million+ DPS to easily defeat the final bosses.
  • Endgame: Your endgame passive tree is dependent on your gear, thus your endgame tree may differ from the example provided. Typically this means acquiring more stats on gear, allowing you to drop passive tree nodes or masteries, such as Spell Suppression or Accuracy.
    • Note: This build is recommended as a 4 Stone League Starter, as it maximises your single target damage on fresh start gear. It is recommended to swap to your endgame map clearing build after acquiring your first 4 Voidstones. That said, additional endgame scaling options are provided as a courtesy for those that want to scale the build further.

Offensive Scaling

  • Your Bow is your most important item for scaling your damage. The #1 Priority is a Bow with as much Flat Elemental Damage as possible. Secondarily, aim for % Increased Critical Strike Chance, % Increased Attack Speed & %Increased Critical Strike Multiplier suffixes
  • Your Quiver is your second most important item for scaling your damage. Similarly, aim for a Quiver with as much Flat Elemental damage as possible. Secondarily, aim for lots of % Increased Damage, % Increased Critical Strike Multiplier & % Increased Attack Speed
  • % Increased Damage: % Increased Projectile Damage, % Attack Damage, % Increased Projectile Speed, % Increased Elemental Damage with Attack Skills, % Increased Damage with Bow Skills
  • % Increased Attack Speed
  • Additional Arrows: Endless Munitions, Master Fletcher, Multishot
  • Flat Elemental Damage to Attacks: Gloves, Rings, Jewellery, Quivers
  • % Increased Critical Strike Chance
  • % Increased Critical Strike Multiplier

Defensive Scaling

  • Sufficient +#% to Fire, Cold and Lightning Resistance to cap your Resistances
  • Evasion Rating
  • Maximum Energy Shield on Hybrid Evasion/Energy Shield Items to use Ghost Dance for burst damage protection
  • Cast when Damage Taken Support + Immortal Call
  • A strong Divine Life Flask with Corrupting Blood Immunity.
  • Any flask with Freeze Immunity

Resistances

You can get Resistances wherever you want and they do not have to be on specific items. Resistances are incredibly important though. DO NOT forget about them when choosing which items to craft or buy! Make sure to check your Resistances in your Character Sheet (C, Defense Tab) whenever you switch your gear around. To learn more about defences in Path of Exile, check out our guide here!

Gear in this section is easier to craft if you obtain a Fractured base. This base 'locks' a modifier, so you can use  Essences  and other crafting methods to obtain a good item. You can search for fractured modifiers on trade by inputting "~fractured (name of modifier)" in the modifier search box. If you want to try out how to craft your final items, the Craft of Exile simulator is an amazing tool to plan them without wasting in-game currency.

Endgame
  • During the campaign, your primary goals are to equip:
    • A High Elemental DPS Bow
    • Any Quiver that improves your DPS
    • 25% to 30% Movement Speed Boots
    • Life & Resistance Gear
    • Flat Damage Jewellery, Amulet or Gloves
  • Acquisition of these items comes from either monster drops, using currency on normal bases, or gambling from Faustus.
  • Upon defeating Act 10 Kitava, use the Gambling Vendor to upgrade any weaker item drops from earlier acts, primarily focusing on basic resistance capping. The life resistance gear you equip here is viable for defeating Eater and Exarch.
  • As you progress through the Atlas, continue to loot Quivers, Rings & Amulets to increase your DPS. Ideally these upgrades are finalised before reaching Red Maps.
  • Around T11 Maps, gamble the gold you've acquired on Bows, searching for a High Elemental DPS Bow, ideally on a Spine Bow or Imperial Bow base.
  • Additionally, ensure your Diamond Flask and Silver Flask are rolled with the % chance to gain a Flask Charge when you deal a Critical Strike modifier, to greatly increase your single target DPS during extended boss fights.

  • Moving from 2 Stones to 4 Stones, your goals are the following:
    • A Higher Elemental DPS Bow
    • 100% Spell Suppression
    • Reaching Level 90+
    • Generally upgraded stats on the rest of your items.
  • Consider using the following Essences to craft Rings, Amulets, Quivers & Gloves:
    • Screaming Essence of Torment
    • Screaming Essence of Anguish
    • Screaming Essence of Suffering
  • Consider using the following Essences to craft a High Elemental DPS Spine Bow:
    • Shrieking Essence of Wrath
    • Shrieking Essence of Anger
    • Screaming Essence of Hatred
  • Gamble for a 5L Hybrid Evasion/Energy Shield body armour if you haven't acquired one already. Note: if you find a 5L Evasion body armour during progression you can equip that as well. However, ensure you have 100 to 300+ total energy shield to effectively use Ghost Dance.
  • When crafting Hybrid Evasion/Energy Shield gear, such as Sadist Garb or Deicide Mask, consider using a Woe Essence to guarantee a flat Energy Shield modifier.
  • To further scale your damage, use the trade site or run Abyss in search of a Stygian Vise and a Searching Eye Jewel.
    • Use a Rage Essence to roll the belt.
    • Use Alterations, Augments, and Regals to roll the jewel. You can also use Harvest reforge.
    • If you don't need to craft a Life or a Resistance Suffix, craft the veiled modifier Regenerate 150 Energy shield per second while a Rare or Unique Enemy is Nearby to improve your ES Recovery.
  • Consider adding Eater or Exarch implicits to your gear with Lesser Eldritch Ember or Lesser Eldritch Ichor as depicted in the embed. This is the last step once you've equipped strong enough items for this stage.

  • Note: This build is recommended as a 4 Stone League Starter, as it maximises your single target damage on fresh start gear. It is recommended to swap to your endgame map clearing build after acquiring your first 4 Voidstones. That said, additional endgame scaling options are provided as a courtesy for those that want to scale the build further.
  • Upgrade your Bow even further, at this stage this typically involves recombinating Blue Spine Bows with T1 Flat Elemental Damage to Attacks Prefixes. The Goal is a Bow with 3x T1 Flat Ele Dmg modifiers. Afterwards you use Prefixes Cannot be Changed and Harvest Reforge Crit to hit a Increased Crit Chance Suffix. Lastly you would craft Attack Speed with the Crafting Bench.
    • Refer to the Recombination Guide for more information.
  • To upgrade your Quiver:
    • Search for a Base with a Fractured Suffix, such as Crit Multi or Projectile Speed
    • Use Deafening Essence of Torment until you hit an item with a second damage suffix, such as Crit Multi or Projectile Speed
    • If your item has 3 Suffixes, then use an Annul to remove one and craft Cannot Roll Attack Modifiers. If you remove your Crit Multi or Projectile Speed roll, then return to Essence spamming.
    • After crafting Cannot Roll Attack Modifiers, Exalt Slam for Life and Increased Damage with Bow Skills. If the rolls aren't high enough, use additional Orbs of Annulment and Exalted Orbs until satisfied.
    • Craft Increased Attack Speed or any other desired modifier to finish the item.
  • For rare items, search for Item Bases with a fractured modifier to significantly improve the crafting process. Typically this means items with fractured Chaos Res, if you value it. However, it's worth noting that Bow Builds usually off screen all enemies without getting hit, thus, its viable to simplify your crafting process by prioritising other modifiers.
  • Blizzard Crowns are acquired from Ritual.
  • Look to equip the following unique items:
    • Hyrri's Truth with 20% Quality from Unstable Catalyst
    • Hyrri's Ire
    • The Taming x2 with 20% Quality from Turbulent Catalyst
    • Dying Sun

Explosive Concoction's high damage scaling and extremely fast mobility make it a great League Start Leveling Build for Beginners, Speedrunners & Experienced Players alike.

Playstyle

Explosive Concoction is both your primary clear and single target ability.

Haste is used while clearing for faster progression, in combination with Flesh and Stone for survivability. For more single target damage enemies are debuffed with Sniper's Mark.

Important Leveling Tips

With experience, the Leveling Progression outlined above is all you need. However, if you're looking for additional guidance, then review the Important Leveling Tips listed below.

General Progression Tips

Weapon & Shield Progression

  • Level 2 Rusted Sword
    • This is your starting weapon!
    • In most scenarios, it's not worth crafting whatsoever.
  • Level 2+ Corroded Blade or Stone Axe or Driftwood Maul
    • Quickly replace your Rusted Swords with a 2H Weapon for a large damage upgrade
  • Level 4+ Early Weapon Swap Shield Charge (Optional)
    • Consider equipping Any Shield in your Weapon Swap to progress quicker. Otherwise, delay this step until Level 8, 10 or 12.
  • Level 8+ Longsword or Tribal Maul or Jade Chopper
    • Keep an eye out for the next tier of 2H Weapons for another large damage upgrade
    • Consider checking the vendor to potentially purchase a base for 1 Transmute
    • Consider using any Alchemy & Essence for more damage
  • Level 12 Poisonous Concoction Shield Swap
    • Any Goathide Buckler or Pine Buckler
  • Level 16+ 6% Movement Speed Shields
    • Any Painted Buckler or Gilded Buckler Base
  • Level 29+ 9% Movement Speed Shields
    • Any War Buckler, Battle Buckler or Crusader Buckler

Listed above is the ideal Explosive Concoction Weapon & Shield breakpoints. The general rule of thumb before swapping to Poisonous Concoction at Level 12 is to equip the Melee Weapon that provides the most DPS. During Act 1, this frequently means an item with tons of Flat Elemental Damage to Attacks.

Additionally, prioritise equipping 2 Iron Rings for more damage. Use any Essence or Alchemy on them to potentially hit a Flat Elemental Damage to Attacks roll. More information on Flat Elemental Damage to Attacks is listed below.

There are three main methods for acquiring item bases: Checking Vendors, Monster Drops & Gambling. However, a special mention needs to be made for the Rogue Exiles in the Broken Bridge. Rogue Exiles drop a full set of gear, ranging from Normal to Magic to Rare; making them a consistent method for acquiring bases, gear upgrades, or items to vendor.

Scaling Damage: Poisonous Concoction

Poisonous Concoction is an unarmed attack skill that throws a bottle that explodes upon touching the ground and has the following tags: Attack, AoE, Chaos, Projectile. Meaning your damage is scaled by the following methods:

  • Increased Projectile Damage, Increased Attack Damage
  • Increased Attack Speed
  • % More Attack Damage: via Precise Technique, Point Blank
  • Additional Projectiles for more "Overlaps" to hit an enemy multiple times: via Volley. Note: you do not use both Volley and Lesser Multiple Projectiles at the same time, as that deletes all of your Life Flask charges.
  • Wither: via Withering Step.
  • Flat Elemental Damage to Attacks: Gear, Auras, Skills.
  • Consuming Life Flask Charges for additional Flat Chaos Damage.

Scaling Damage: Explosive Concoction

Explosive Concoction is an unarmed attack skill that throws a bottle that explodes upon touching the ground and has the following tags: Attack, AoE, Fire, Cold, Lightning, Projectile. Meaning your damage is scaled by the following methods:

  • Increased Projectile Damage, Increased Attack Damage
  • Increased Attack Speed
  • % More Attack Damage: via Precise Technique, Point Blank
  • Additional Projectiles for more "Overlaps" to hit an enemy multiple times: via Volley + Lesser Multiple Projectiles at Level 28 and Greater Volley + Volley at Level 38 or higher.
  • Flat Elemental Damage to Attacks: Gear, Auras, Skills.
  • Consuming Utility Flask Charges
    • Added Cold Damage if charges were consumed from a Sapphire Flask
    • Added Lighting Damage if charges were consumed from a Topaz Flask
    • % to Damage over Time Multiplier with Ignite if charges were consumed from a Ruby Flask

Flat Damage, Essences & Crafts

Flat Elemental Damage to Attacks is extremely important & crucial to acquire to continuously scale your damage throughout progression. Flat Elemental Damage to Attacks can roll or be crafted onto the following slots:

  • Rings
  • Amulet
  • Gloves

There are other sources of Flat Damage, such as Searching Eye Jewels, but they aren't consistently available to consider for campaign progression.

In addition to Checking Vendors & Identifying Monster Drops; you can use the following currency orbs to modify items you find:

  • Essences
  • Orb of Alchemy, Orb of Binding, Chaos Orb
  • Orb of Transmutation, Orb of Augmentation, Orb of Alteration

Essences are of particular interest, since they allow you to force a Flat Elemental Damage Modifier. See the list below for more information:

  • Amulets, Rings & Gloves:
    • Cold: Weeping Essence of Suffering, Wailing Essence of Suffering, Screaming Essence of Suffering
    • Fire: Wailing Essence of Anguish, Screaming Essence of Anguish
    • Lightning: Muttering Essence of Torment, Weeping Essence of Torment, Wailing Essence of Torment, Screaming Essence of Torment
  • Note: Only Essences that naturally spawn during the Campaign are listed. There are two additional tiers of Essences available after Screaming, that can be found in Maps or from special league mechanics.

Keep an eye out for Unique enemies that drop specific Essences. They only drop Essences the first time you click them; so consider resetting the zone until you spawn an Essence you desire. Resetting a zone, is only recommended in Act 3 Docks, if you are simultaneously farming XP.

The last method for acquiring Flat Damage on Gear is the Crafting Bench. However, Crafts have to be unlocked by finding them in various zones, refer to the table below for their location.

Act-by-Act Progression Tips

Act 1

  • In the Submerged Passageway, place a Portal at the Bridge, to more quickly enter Flooded Depths Later.
  • In the Upper Prison, search for a Chemist's Strongbox, to potentially acquire Quicksilvers.

Act 2

  • In the Old Fields, place a Portal outside of the Den Entrance. Then travel to Crossroads, take the Waypoint to town, and back through your Portal to enter the Den. Complete the Great White Beast Quest to acquire a Quicksilver.
  • In the Broken Bridge, search for and defeat the 3 Exiles beside the waypoint. Keep an eye out for item upgrades, jewellery, and gear to vendor.
  • There are many Breaches in Act 2. The Breach in Riverways is often found on the way and can be a great source of extra experience and items while progressing.

Act 3

  • Solaris 2
    • Important: Acquire the Fire Damage Craft in the Room beside Lady Dialla
  • Lunaris 2
    • Important: Acquire the Lightning Damage Craft in the Upper Left of Piety's Arena
  • In Docks you can find a Unique enemy that drops a guaranteed Essence. They will only drop Essences the first time you click them; so consider resetting the zone until you spawn an Essence you desire.
  • Library
    • If you complete the Library to purchase extra support gems earlier, you can skip Act 6 Twilight Strand quest later.

Act 4

  • Get Level 32 before entering Kaom's Stronghold to avoid severe XP Penalties.
  • Crystal Veins
    • Acquire the Cold Damage craft beside the waypoint.

Act 5

  • The Chamber of Innocence has many guaranteed Magic Monster packs. It's strongly recommended to go out of your way to farm them.
  • Before entering the Reliquary to complete the Passive Skill Point quest, place a portal outside of the Cathedral Rooftop entrance, to progress faster.

Act 6

  • Lily Roth
    • If you completed the Library quest earlier and already purchased your extra support gems. You can skip the quest to full clear Twilight Strand.
  • In the Beacon, while progressing the fuel pylons, consider placing a Safety Portal as the enemies that spawn can occasionally be very dangerous.

Act 7

  • Chamber of Sins 1
    • Acquire the T2 Lightning Damage Craft beside the waypoint.
  • After defeating Gruest, and progressing forward to the Causeway; consider placing a portal near the Dread Thicket entrance if you see it, to progress faster.
  • Causeway
    • Acquire the T2 Cold Damage Craft beside the waypoint.
  • Dread Thicket
    • Acquire the T2 Fire Damage Craft near Dreadstone.

Act 8

  • Upon returning to the Harbour Bridge to complete the act, reset the zone by Ctrl + Clicking the entrance for more experience along the way.

Act 9

  • Consider farming Blood Aqueduct for more experience and gear before progressing to maps.
  • Consider rolling a Bleed Removal Flask and using Grace in the Belly of the Beast.

Act 10

  • Upon entering the Ravaged Square, place a portal near the entrance, before travelling to the Ravaged Square waypoint. Then take the waypoint back to town, and go through your portal and head south, in search of the The Control Blocks to defeat Vilenta.

That said, the other Champion ascendancy nodes offer limited Explosive Concoction scaling options when completing the Atlas, thus Slayer becomes the optimal choice for Mapping.

Generally, during the campaign, it's almost always better to opt for more damage, making a Topaz Flask the best choice.

    Most likely you looted a weapon with the Auto-equip option enabled. Simply unequip the weapon you accidently equipped to continue.

    Additionally, consider disabling the Auto-equip option to prevent the issue from occurring. That said, it's generally recommended to keep Auto-equip enabled, as it saves time during progression.

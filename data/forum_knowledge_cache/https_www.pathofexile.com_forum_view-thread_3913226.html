You can get Iron Reflexes from the tree to become tankier. Evasion isn't necessarily tanky; the damage you don't evade will kill you because you have no health. Armor will reduce the average damage you receive. The best scenario would be to have a lot of armor and evasion, but this node is a good band-aid for your build. Your flasks won't stack the % inc Evasion, just use the jade with evasion and get something else in the diamond, like % inc armor or crit during the flask effect.

But I think the most important thing now is to become a Slayer, and I would recommend the following points: Masterful Form and Brutal Fervor + Endless Hunger. I haven't tested Huntleader Bloodline, but if you want, you can even go full phys damage with Bane of Legends as an eighth point option instead of Bloodline. If you have the money, it would be interesting to get a pair of Forbidden Flesh + Forbidden Flame with the Headsman node, which is super good.

One option I like to use with this Slayer Flicker setup is the Arns Anguish Cloth Belt. It won't give you the tankiness of End Charge, but it greatly increases damage.

Honestly, almost everyone who plays Flicker Strike knows they're going to die all the time, so most people use Cast on Death + Portal to make it more comfortable.

With Flicker Strike, the important thing is to deal enough damage to prevent the enemy from hitting you. Or you can make a version with Svalinn and try to block. I did, and it's not that much better than the normal version; it's good, but it's the same story: the hit that gets past the block will kill you.

Another thing I noticed is that you don't use Herald of Ice. I personally can't play Flicker Strike without Herald; the clear is better, and with a skin, it's much cooler.

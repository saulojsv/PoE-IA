Kinetic Blast of Clustering Necromancer League Starter for Path of Exile Mirage 3.28

by Legi
Mirage 3.28Last Updated: March 25, 2026

The Kinetic Blast of Clustering Necromancer League Starter is a clearspeed powerhouse that covers your screen with explosions of kinetic force.

Kinetic Blast of Clustering is an alternate quality version of Kinetic Blast that fires a single payload projectile which explodes into multiple explosions when hitting its target. It also has built-in damage scaling which streamlines your character building process.

The Necromancer, which is usually utilized as a go-to minion ascendancy, offers you a massive amount of increased Attack Speed through clever use of Corpse Pact in conjunction with multiple Spellslinger setups.
This allows you to amplify the build's clear speed and movement through Shield Charge immensely.

Early on, your character focuses on scaling Life, Evasion rating, Block and Spell chance to avoid most of the damage from attacks and spell hits. Tempest Shield and Bone Offering help to cap your Block and Spell Block, while Corpse Pact chills enemies around the corpses you spawn withDesecrate.

This build doesn't require any Unique items to function altough greatly benefits from them and is viable to be played in SSF if you are detemined enough to get past early game hurdles.

Resistances

You do not need specific resistances on any item, just get the required total resistances amongst all gear combined. You need to cap your Resistances at 75% unless otherwise specified in the guide!

Personal Choices
  • If you are starting in a fresh league, it's highly recommend to make a Ranger mule to get a free Momentum Support, buying an Iron ring and checking the vendor for items with desirable links. You are looking for BBB, BBG and BBR.
  • Kinetic Bolt is avaiable at level 1 and is your main Skill for the first 12 levels.
  • While fighting Rare or Unique monsters, use Holy Flame Totem and Flame Wall to supplement your damage. Place Flame Wall so that your character and totems are opposite your target so that you both benefit.
  • After reaching Cavern of Anger, buy Kinetic Fusillade from the vendor and use it as your main skill. Make sure to use Volley Support.
  • Use Shield Charge to move around quickly.
  • If you have a spare Orb of Transmutation, buy a 2nd Added Lightning Damage Support and link it with your Holy Flame Totem.
  • After killing Brutus, buy Precision, as Witch's Accuracy is very low early on.
  • Kinetic Fusillade has a limit of 12 hovering projectiles. To maximize your single target damage, you want to attack 3 to 4 times (Number of Projectiles/12) and wait for the skill to release.
  • In Act 2, add Herald of Thunder and Herald of Ice for added elemental damage.
  • In Act 3, add Elemental Weakness and cast it on tankier targets.
  • Don't forget to kill General Gravicious to acquire Kinetic Blast as the quest reward.
  • You will need around 10 Orb of Transmutation to buy Gems and craft flat damage on all available prefixes. Try to save as many as you can to utilize the crafting bench.
  • After you reach level 31, you want to complete the Library, which grants you access to a gem vendor.
  • Take Lightning Penetration Support as a quest reward and buy: Fork Support, Ballista Totem Support, Faster Attacks Support, Blood Rage, Sniper's Mark, Focused Ballista Support and Spellslinger. Make sure to level Ballista Totem Support in your off-hand weapon.
  • Replace Elemental Weakness with Sniper's Mark.
  • You can switch to Kinetic Blast as your main skill and continue progressing.
  • Replace your Holy Flame Totem with the Kinetic Bolt Ballista setup.
  • Link your Spellslinger with Flame Wall to automate it. At level 34, add Hydrosphere to the same Spellslinger link.
  • Hydrosphere applies Exposure to the target, reducing its Elemental Resistance and creating a sphere that can be hit with Kinetic Blast to increase single target damage.
  • After completing Lilly Roth's quest in Act 6, replace Hydrosphere with Tornado in the Spellslinger setup and cast Hydrosphere manually. Tornado has the same function as Hydrosphere, and stacking them together helps create explosion overlaps for Kinetic Blast.
  • Link Sniper's Mark with Mark On Hit Support to automate it.
  • Use this gem setup until Cruel Labyrinth.
  • Cruel Labyrinth is a very important part of progression and a major skill swap.
  • After acquiring your 2nd ascendancy point, respec into Corpse Pact This Ascendancy Skill is the de-facto reason to play KBoC on a Necromancer.
  • Add a 2nd Spellslinger setup consisting of: Desecrate, Volatile Dead (Later Volatile Dead of Seething), Spell Cascade Support.
    • Keep all active gems in your Spellslinger setups at level 1, excluding Bone Offering, as they gain additional mana costs with levels without providing any useful benefits.
  • To fully benefit from Corpse Pact's increased Attack and Cast speed, you need to create and consume a lot of corpses. Volatile Dead and Bone Offering are the best tools for the job.
  • Acquire Assassin's Mark and level it in your off-hand weapon.
  • Proceed to Merciless Labyrinth.
  • Around level 60, you have enough skill points to switch to your main skill, Kinetic Blast of Clustering.
  • To optimize single target damage, try not to stand exactly on top of the target you are fighting. Position yourself slightly to the side to maximize the explosion overlaps.
  • Add Sigil of Power and cast it while fighting Bosses. If possible, try to stand in the Sigil's area, as it provides reduced damage taken when fully charged.
  • Discard Sniper's Mark and add Assassin's Mark. If possible, try to get at least 10% gem quality for it, as this lets you generate Power Charges in single target situations.
    It is the best way to do so however alternatively you can use Power Charge On Critical Support or Wand Mastery until you get the gem quality.
  • Add Frost Shield. It consumes a portion of your energy shield to provide a defensive buff while you stand in it, taking the portion of incoming damage instead of you.
  • Kinetic Blast of Clustering provides a huge amount of flat physical damage proportional to your Mana.
  • At this stage, convert all your physical damage to lightning using Physical to Lightning Support and a Lightning Mastery on the skill tree.
  • Continue using Sigil of Power and Hydrosphere on tanky Rares and Bosses.
  • Add Lightning Penetration Support as soon as you can.
  • Remember to keep all active gems in your Spellslinger setups at level 1, excluding Bone Offering, as they gain additional mana costs with levels without providing any useful benefits.
  • You want to switch to to chaos damage profile AS SOON AS YOU CAN so don't delay getting your Overwhelming Malice Medium Cluster Jewel.
  • After acquiring either a Medium Cluster Jewel Critical Strike or Large Cluster Jewel Chaos Damage Cluster Jewel with Overwhelming Malice, switch to this variant as soon as possible.
  • Overwhelming Malice provides Unholy Might, a buff that fully converts physical damage to Chaos Damage and applies Wither, which is a massive stacking debuff for Chaos Damage. You can also consider using Withering Step for more wither consistency if you'd like but the socket pressure on the build is quite high.
  • Swap out Physical to Lightning Support and Lightning Penetration Support for Void Manipulation Support and Sacred Wisps Support for increased Chaos Damage and wisps copying your attacks.

Build is somewhat flexsible so you can make some small choices and adjustments..
Because of the double Spellslinger the socket pressure is high and they are quite limited however they can still be very impactful.

Defensive Variations

  • Endurance charges are important part of the build as we have almost no physical mitigation.
    They synergise well with Cast when Damage Taken Support + Immortal Call, mostly in maping situations. If you consistent charge generation on bosses, use Enduring Composure from armour Small Cluster Jewel or Exarch/Warlord Body Armour implicit/explicit.
  • Keep using Frost Shield even in lategame setups, its very good.

Offensive Variations

  • Add Withering Step
  • Get +1 curese corruption on the amulet or spec Whispers of Doom and handcast Despair

It's worth noting, you can include a lot more utility on your character if you optimise the Mirage league mechanic currency, Coin of Knowledge, Coin of Skill & Coin of Power. Adding level 1 support gem to Immortal Call, Shield Charge or Assassin's Mark can be strong.

How to Ascend

Learn how to find the Trials of Ascendancy and complete The Lord's Labyrinth to earn your Ascensions with our Labyrinth Guide!

  • First, Commander of Darkness is an Aura that grants you +30% All Resistances and a sizable amount of increased damage. It's a great early game choice that helps you cap your resistances. Respec this after completing Cruel Labyrinth.
  • Second, Plaguebringer grants you increased Area of Effect after you consume a corpse and reduces enemy damage and defense when they're near a corpse.
  • Corpse Pact is the most important Ascendancy Node and the one that the build revolves around. It grants you 4% increased Attack and Cast Speed for each corpse consumed recently. On top of that, it Shocks and Chills enemies near the corpses you've created. With the correct gem setup, you can fully stack this buff by consuming hundreds of corpses. It's worth noting that Shock and Chill granted by this Ascendancy also scales with Ailment Effect from your gear and passive tree.
  • The fourth Ascendancy is very flexible. You have many different choices depending on what you want to achieve. You can either choose one of the remaining unallocated points or a Bloodline Ascendancy. This is done by defeating the corresponding Boss and pressing the shrine afterwards.
    • Early on, Essence Glutton is the best candidate. It provides a ton of Energy Shield and Mana Regeneration, making it a strong defensive option. The Mana Regeneration part is especially handy to ramp your Corpse Pact Attack Speed before engaging Bosses.
    • The strongest offensive option is Huntleader, which is acquired by defeating Farrul in the Menagerie. The buff from the Cat minions is active practically permanently with how rapidly Kinetic Blast of Clustering hits targets.
    • Alternatively, you can choose Oshabi's Ascendancy. The small pathing point Rage towards The Wild Bear grants you 2 Rage on hit. When fully stacked, Rage provides up to 30% more attack damage and its uptime of fully stacked rage is relatively high compared to a Glove implicit. The other remaining point is small pathing point Minion Damage, Mana towards Essence Glutton.
  • Major Pantheon: Soul of the Brine King, which provides psuedo Stun and Freeze immunity.
  • Minor pantheon: Soul of Aberrath, which prevents damage from burning ground effects.
  • Once you optain endgame gear pieces and become immune to elemental ailments and stun consider switching to Soul of Lunaris of its defensive benefits.

How to Progress

After finishing the campaign, you begin interacting with the Atlas of Worlds. Learn more about how to progress your Atlas check out our Atlas progression guide! Or head over to our Boss section to learn how to defeat the Eldritch Horrors you encounter while exploring the Atlas.

  • Look to solve Intelligence, Dexterity and Strength requirements on gear. If you find yourself short, you can spec into Attribute Notables as a temporary solution.
    • You can use Faustus to respec if a new skill or gear upgrade leaves you unable to use your skills/items.
  • Before transitioning into the mana-stacking Kinetic Blast of Clustering setup, you will utilize various Wand Attack skills during the Campaign. Swap between Kinetic Bolt, Kinetic Fusillade and Kinetic Blast whenever they are available. Each of these skills have almost identical damage scaling, so you can switch between them effortlessly using the Campaign trees. You want to focus on taking powerful Wand clusters like Wandslinger and Fusillade.
  • The tree provides huge amount of increased damage from the Witch starting nodes and elemental clusters. Make sure to focus on crafting flat Lightning Damage to Attacks on your Wand and Jewelry.
  • From Act 6 onwards, try to limit gambling altogether, as you will need gold for your Cruel Labyrinth Ascendany re-spec.
  • After acquiring your Transfigured Gem, remove generic nodes like Searing Heat and Snowforged in favor of passive points that scale mana, like Deep Thoughts.
  • After completing Merciless Labyrinth and allocating Mistress of Sacrifice, start focusing on specing Block and Spell Block nodes.
    Attack Block and Evasion is are your main defensive layers so make sure to not neglect them.
  • Once you acquire your Overwhelming Malice Cluster, that's all you need to change to Chaos Damage profile. Unspec all Lightning Damage passive points like Tempest Blast and Arcing Blows in favour of Heart of Darkness or missing % life nodes like Cruel Preparation.
  • There is a lot of flexibility when it comes to last 10 passive points of the character. Adjust accordingly to the content you are farming.

Offensive Scaling

  • Flat Mana and %Mana. This is your main damage scaler.
  • Critical Strike Chance and Critical Strike Multiplier.
  • Increased Spell Damage. Kinetic Blast of Clustering converts %Spell Damage to Attack Damage at 200% effectiveness.
  • Increased Attack Speed
  • Increased Chaos/Physical/Wand Damage

Defensive Scaling

  • Sufficient +#% to Fire, Cold and Lightning Resistance to cap your Resistances.
  • Block and Spell Block, up to 75% each.
    Attack block is your main defensive layer so try to cap it as soon as you can.
  • Endurance Charges
  • + to Maximum Life and %increased Maximum Life
  • + to Maximum Energy Shield
  • +#% chance to Suppress Spell Damage.
  • Energy Shield on Block (Shaper Shield Suffix)

Resistances

You can get Resistances wherever you want and they do not have to be on specific items. Resistances are incredibly important though. DO NOT forget about them when choosing which items to craft or buy! Make sure to check your Resistances in your Character Sheet (C, Defense Tab) whenever you switch your gear around. To learn more about defences in Path of Exile, check out our guide here!

Item Bases

You want to focus on using Hybrid Evasion/Energy Shield bases. The Kinetic Blast Necromancer utilizes mostly Dexterity and Intelligence gems, so it's much easier to acquire correct combinations of sockets. Evasion provides a chance to avoid hits and synergizes well with attack block while Energy Shield increased the total damage you can take from all sources, excluding Chaos damage.

In the early stages of progression, your character will be life-based, utilizing evasion to avoid as much damage as possible. The hit mitigation is not very good and unfortunately there is not much we can do without sacrificing excesive amouts of damage.
You want to use item bases like Phantom Boots, Phantom Mitts, Torturer's Mask and Sanguine Raiment.
Its difficult to equip Necrotic Armour as the dexterity requirement is very steep.
Your Body Armour and Shield are the most impactful defensive items so it is really important to have correct affixes on them.
While chestpiece covers the biggest slice of Evasion and Energy Shield needs, Shield is the gear piece that is responsible for the chance to block attacks and spells. Try to avoid having life or life regeneration on the body armour to benefit from 15% increased maxium life Life Mastery

Wand

Kinetic Wand is by far the best base. It is the fastest-attacking wand in the game which is the most impactful factor and multiplier combined with all the global % increased attack speed, mainly provided by Corpse Pact.
Secound best type of base is Prophecy Wand. Avoid bases like Demon's Horn.

Key Items

  • Overwhelming Malice Cluster Jewel
    This is the most important item of the build. You want to acquire it as soon as you can. It allows the build to fully convert its damage to Chaos, which provides very powerful scaling vectors such as Wither. In general, monsters in Path of Exile have lower Chaos Resistance than Elemental Resistance.
  • Shield with high %Attack Block Chance
    You want to aim for as high %Chance to Block Attack Damage on your Shield as possible. It's the key defensive item of the build. You want to aim for 40%+ if possible. It can guaranteed by using Deafening Essence of Loathing on a base with high Block Chance like Titanium Spirit Shield or Transfer-attuned Spirit Shield.
    • Focus on the % Chance to Block first, then life and elemental resistances. Offensive stats like % increased Spell Damage are mostly a luxury.
